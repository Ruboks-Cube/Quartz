# Problems
## Connor Family
There are 9 children in the Connor Family, their parents buy 99 goldfish and 72 angel fish, if each child receives the same number of fish, how many fish would they each receive?

We could divide after we count all the fish, so we would do ${99+72}$ first. But then we can notice that both 99 and 72 are divisible by 9, so we could first divide the goldfish among the children which would be $\frac{99}{9}=11$ and then the angel fish $\frac{72}{9}=8$. Since each child gets 11 goldfish and 8 angelfish, the total would be $9(11+8)$

Running the distributive property forwards would be $11*9+8*9$, running it backwards we take the common factor of 9 out of each term and put it by itself: $9(11+8)$.

Factoring gives us the answer much more quickly in this problem. We know we have to divide all the fish by 9, and that there are 99+72 fish, but we can factor out the 9 to get $\frac{{9(11+8)}}{9}$, the 9's cancel out and we are left with $11+8=19$ fish for each child.
