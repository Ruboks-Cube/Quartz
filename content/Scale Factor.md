# In ratios
[[Ratios come in groups]], sometimes we need to find out how many groups we have in a certain [[Ratio]], when we do that, we can multiply the number of groups we have (which is our scale of ratio) by the value or [[part of a group]] we need

The scale of a [[Ratio]] is what we multiply the relative numbers of a ratio by to get the total value we want. We find it using [[Linear Equation]]s often. 

x is the scale we multiply the ratio by to get the value. Ratio's are relative numbers, to get the total we want, we get the scale (x) with the unit, gallons, dollars. To get this scale, you make an equation based on the information given since finding what times the ratio equals something will give you the scale, which is x. 

Like here, ratio to get a specific shade of orange paint is 2:5:4, white paint to red paint to yellow paint. If we need 6 gallons of orange paint, how much paint will we need of each color. To find this, we do 2x+5x+4x = 6, 2 times some number of gallons, + 5 times some number of gallons + 4 times some number of gallons, will give us 6 gallons. Since 2,5,4 are relative numbers since they are a ratio, x has the value of gallons. Since we have 11 gallons in total, 11x=6, x=6/11, meaning multiplying 11 by 6/11 will get us 6 gallons.
