#math/algebra/inequality/equations/Ratios_Proportions_Rates , [[Ratio]]


- A unit rate is when one [[part of a group\|Part of a ratio]] is $1$ (a unit). We get the unit rate by pretending a part of our [[Ratio]] is already 1
	- ? so now we ask ourselves, what [[Scale Factor]] we multiply the other part(s) to keep the ratio [[equivalent]]? 
	- ! We multiply it by the [[Reciprocal]] of our original number that we turned to 1. Because think about it, if we have $x:y$ and we want $y=1$ we just have $\frac{x}{y}=\frac{?}{1}$. We divide $y$ by $y$ so we also divide $x$ by $y$ to keep the [[Fraction]] [[equivalent]]. 


- The Unit Rate of [[slope]] itself is really weird, because the unit rate is just itself? Slope is ${\frac{\triangle y}{\triangle x}}$. We get the unit rate by doing this: $\frac{{\triangle y}}{\triangle x}=\frac{?}{1}$. We could either "multiply both sides by $\triangle x$" ORRRR we can do the fun way which is to realize that since the [[Denominator]] on the right side is 1 we must've divided the left side's denominator by $\triangle x$. This means that to keep the [[Fraction]] - [[equivalent]] we divide the [[Numerator]] ($\triangle y$) by $\triangle x$ too. So since these are both variables, all we are left with is $\frac{\frac{{\triangle y}}{{\triangle x}}}{1}$ on the right side. WHICH GUESS WHAT just equals $\frac{{\triangle y}}{\triangle x}$. So the unit rate of ${\frac{\triangle y}{\triangle x}}$ is just itself.
	- ~ another way to see this is to see it as a ratio
		- Change in y : change in x
		- Change in y: 1
			- ! Oh wow we divided change in x by change in x to get 1 so lets divide change in y by 1 too!
		- We get change in y over change in x : 1
