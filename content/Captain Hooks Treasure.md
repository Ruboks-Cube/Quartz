A niche [[Word Problem]]
### Part 1

This problem is an introduction to a problem in [[algebra#2. X marks the spot\|The 2nd chapter of introduction to algebra by AOPS]]
![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240406123036.png]]
It might take a while, but what he's describing is the number of paces you take when facing west from the palm tree to go to the treasure.

He says, "Think ye of the number that is seventeen more than the quotient when six times the number of paces to the treasure is divided by 2"
Here the "number of paces" could be a [[variable math\|variable]], lets call it "x"

$17+\frac{{6*x}}{2}$ is what we have so far. Then he says "This number be equal to the sum when 12 more than 9 minus 15 is added 4 times the number of paces to the treasure"

This means $12+(9-15)+4(x)$
and he says that these two [[Expression\|Expressions]] are equal to each other.

$17+\frac{{6*x}}{2}=12+9-15+4(x)$

This is a [[Linear Equation]]! 
### Part 2
![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240417203148.png]]
- The first step is to translate this to math!
- "3 times the sum of the number of northerly steps and the number of easterly steps is 4 more than 4 times the number of northernly steps"
	- What a mouthful, if we let $n$ be north steps and $e$ be easternly steps we get this:
		- $3(n+e)=4(n)-4$
	- "when ye multiply by five the number two less than the number of northerly steps, ye get the number that is two more than seven times the number of easterly steps"
		- $5(n-2)=7e+2$
	- Now, we have two [[equations\|Equations]] that both have 2 variables. In order to find the value's or n and e, the northern and easterly steps, we have to find the values of $n$ and $e$ that fit both equations.
	- This is an example of a [[system of equations]]
