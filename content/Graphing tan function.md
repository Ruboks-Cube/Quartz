#math/graphing #math/geometry/2d/shape/Triangle/trigonometry 

- Graphing [[Tangent]] is a lot different than graphing [[cosine]] or [[sine]]
	- Remember that on [[The Unit Circle]], [[Tangent]] is $\frac{y}{x}$
	- What does this mean for the graph?  
		- It means that it has [[Asymptote\|Asymptotes]] because when $x$ is $0$ on The Unit Circle that means that it’s **undefined** and therefore we put an asymptote
		- The usual place is $\frac{\pi}{2}$ and $-\frac{\pi}{2}$ because those are where [[cosine]] equals 0
			- This means the [[period]] for $\tan$ is [[pi]] (distance between $\frac{\pi}{2}$ and $-\frac{\pi}{2}$ is $\pi$)
		- Then we just consider what the values of $\tan$ are
		- $\tan(0)=0$
		- $\tan(45)=1$, $\tan\left( \frac{\pi}{4} \right)=1$ (increasing)
		- $\tan(-45)=-1$, $\tan\left( -\frac{\pi}{4} \right)=-1$ (decreasing)
- A tan function could be lookin like this:
	$y=a\tan(b(\theta-c))+d$
	- $a$ corresponds with the [[amplitude]]
	- $b$ corresponds with the [[period]]
	- The distance between the [[Asymptote\|Asymptotes]] is $\frac{\pi}{b}$
	- $c$ is the [[Frame shift]]
- $x=0$ is not always $d$, plug in $x=0$ to find what the y [[intercept]] is or the [[midpoint]] between the [[Asymptote\|asymptotes]]  
- The [[Frame shift\|phase shift]] in tan functions and [[graphing cotan function]] move the [[Asymptote\|asymptotes]] 

> [!danger] PROBLEM: Finding the asymptotes of a tan function
> What are the asymptotes of $\tan(2x+\pi)+1$
> - To graph a tan function, find the asymptotes first.
> - The normal asymptotes are $-\frac{\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2}$
> - Simplify $\tan(2x+\pi)+1$ to $\tan_{2}\left( x+\frac{\pi}{2} \right)+1$
> - The [[Frame shift]]  is $\frac{\pi}{2}$ left
> - The period is $\frac{\pi}{2}$
> - The distance between the asymptotes shrink by half first, so we have $-\frac{\pi}{4}$ and $\frac{\pi}{4}$
> - Apply the frame shift, $\left( -\frac{3\pi}{4} \right)$ and $\left( -\frac{\pi}{4} \right)$ are the asymptotes
