#math #math/geometry , [[The Elements\|Euclidian Geometry]], [[analytical geometry]], [[Geometry]], [[algebra]], [[proof]]

- Many proofs don't rely on numerical information about positions of [[point\|points]] or [[orientation\|orientations]] of [[Line Segment\|line segments]]
	- Instead, proofs use relationships between [[Line Segment\|sides]] and [[Angles]]. 
	- [[congruent\|congruence]], [[parallel\|parallelism]], [[Perpendicular\|perpendicularity]] are a few relationships used
		- EG: [[2 isosceles right triangles with common hypotenuse form a square]]
- It's also possible to do proofs with [[analytical geometry]] 
	- You can prove properties of geometric shapes with [[algebra]]
	- You can explain how different positions of [[point\|points]] ([[ordered pair\|coordinates]]) are related. How orientations of [[line\|lines]] ([[slope\|slopes]]) are related
