#math/algebra/inequality/equations/polynomial

lets say we have something like $2x^2+7x+3$, teacher says multiplying 2 and 3 then finding what sums to 7 will let us find what two numbers to group it in. 

This is called the stub method because I'm calling it that. Here is why it works:

$(sx+t)(ux+b)$ is what a [[factored form quadratic]] would look like. If we [[double distribution\|Double distribute]] this we get $sux^2+bsx+tux+tb$. If we [[Factoring\|factor]] $x$ from 2 middle terms it will be $sux^2+(bs+tu)x+tb$, you can see here that there will always be a group available, so we can always factor out stuff when we group it.

We have $sux^2+bsx+tux+tb$.

We can group the terms to be $(sux^2+bsx)+(tux+tb)$, then we can factor out $sx$ and $t$ to get $sx(ux+b)+t(ux+b)$, and factor out the whole expression of $(ux+b)$ to get $(sx+t)(ux+b)$, and there you have it, the proof :)
