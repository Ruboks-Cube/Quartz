# What are they
- The number line lets us represent [[Positive Numbers]], [[Negative Number\|Negative numbers]], [[zero, 0\|zero]], and any [[Real Numbers\|Real number]] visually, including [[decimal\|Decimals]]
- The number line also helps us solve some basic [[Inequalities]]

- Here is an example of a number line: ![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240522204747.png]]
	- You can see the [[Negative Number\|Negative numbers]], [[Positive Numbers]], and [[zero, 0\|Zero]]. If we wanted decimals/ [[Fraction\|Fractions]], we put it between the [[Whole Number\|Whole Numbers]]. 
	- So yes, a number line is a bunch of numbers below a line that are in order, like one of those lists in [[Counting]]. The nice thing is, that the scale of the number line (how much we count by, like 2's, 4's, the distance between each number) and where the numberline starts and ends, is also up to us, like a list. 
	- The arrows on either end indicate the number line goes forever. [[line]]

	- The distance between a number and 0 is called [[Absolute Value]]

- We can plot numbers on the number line: ![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240525091643.png]]
	- Here you can see that the number $4.2$ is plotted

> [!NOTE] Note
> We can use the number line to see relationships between numbers, just like the [[Cartesian coordinate system]].
> We can also use it to see [[addition]] and [[subtraction]], adding 3 is taking 3 steps to the right, subtracting 5 is taking 5 steps to the left.

- [[Rene Descartes]] great insight for Analytic Geometry was adding another dimension to the number line, creating the [[Cartesian coordinate system]]





# Problems
## key points
- ~ [[Absolute Value]] is always [[Positive Numbers\|Positive]]
- ! To find the midpoint between 2 numbers, we just take the difference and half it. This gives us the number we add to get the midpoint.
## 8.1
![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240525183355.png]]
- ~ Solution:
	- We can use the [[Number Line]] or simple [[subtraction]] to see that $9-(-3)=12$ units to the right of $-3$ until it hits $9$
	- $ Here we are simply taking the difference of $9$ and $-3$. [[Finding the distance between 2 numbers]]
	- ! Similarly, if $a>b$ then we have $a-b$ steps to the right of $b$. This is because taking the [[Finding the distance between 2 numbers\|Difference of a and b]] steps from $b$ to the right gives $b+(a-b)=a$. Adding $a-b$ represents taking the steps to get $a$. 
	- So, $9$ is $12$ steps to the right of ${} -3$, so the midpoint will be $\frac{12}{2}=6$ units to the right of $-3$ ![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240525185129.png]] which is $3$
	- ~ We take $\frac{1}{2}$ of the amount of total steps to get to the middle. This is different and similar from the [[Median]] at the same time which is very very weird. Here, we have an even number of Steps from $-3$ to $9$, meaning there is $12$ steps to get from $-3$ DIRECTLY TO $9$, so $-3$ is not included in this list, but $9$ is. However, this means that there is $11$ numbers BETWEEN $-3$ and $9$, so the midpoint will be $-3+6$ because the $6$th number would be the median in a list of 11. 
	- $ Just remember to half the amount of steps needed then add.
	- ! Notice how the mean and the midpoint/median is the same! The [[Mean]] is the 2 numbers added divided by $2$, $\frac{{9-3}}{2}=3$, since this is a 2 number list, the mean will just be the [[Median]] since 2 is even, and the [[Median]] for a even list is the [[Mean]] of the middle 2 numbers.
