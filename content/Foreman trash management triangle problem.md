#math/geometry/2d/shape/Triangle #math/geometry , [[compass]], [[Triangle]], [[ASA angle side angle]]


- Take a look at this 
	![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240711220353.png|Diagram|200]]
	- As a part of landfill management waste department staff need to keep accurate records of the amount of material in each dump so they can determine the rate at which each one is filling up and schedule the excavation of new pits
		- The company has a set of poles on the far end of every pit.
			- Each pole has a red stripe marking 4 feet off ground level and the scale for feet starts at 8 feet off the ground.
		- The foreman can drive up to the opening of the pit and use a [[compass]] (for measuring angles) mounted/aligned to the level 4 feet off the ground to determine how many feet of debris the pit can take before it fills to ground level
		- This method relies on [[Triangle]]--[[congruent\|congruence]]
- The 2 triangles in the diagram are congruent by [[ASA angle side angle]] criterion. $\angle DAB$ in the upper [[Triangle]] is equal to the measure of $\angle CAB$ in the lower triangle. 
	- The triangles share a [[Line Segment\|side]]
	- The triangles have [[adjacent]] [[Right Angle\|Right angles]] because the pole is [[vertical]] and the compass alignment is [[Perpendicular]] to the pole
- Because the 2 triangles are [[congruent]] $BC=BD$ to find $CG$ usually the foreman would have to subtract 4ft aka subtracting $BG$ but they don't have to (yipeee) because the poles are marked in a way that accounts for this difference.
