#math/algebra/inequality/equations/polynomial 

- [[factored form]], [[Factor]]
- we have to understand that as we get closer to a [[root]] and eventually cross the root on the $x$ axis, the only thing that changes signs is the factor with that root
	- ! This matters because the only thing that matters is the [[Factor]] of the [[root]]
		- e.g. if a [[Factor]] is $(x-3)$ when we go from $x=2$ to ${} x=4 {}$ ONLY $(x-3)$ will change signs from being a negative to a positive because all the other roots aren’t “crossed” 
			- If another root is like $(x-5)$ that will not go from negative to positive (yet) because we haven’t went past $x=5$ which is the [[root]]
