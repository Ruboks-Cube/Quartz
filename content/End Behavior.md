#math/algebra/inequality/equations/polynomial #math/graphing 
> [!info] INFO: The [[term]] with the [[degree of polynomial\|degree]] gets the say on End Behavior
> - EVEN DEGREE
> 	- Think about a really big positive number → positive y value
> 	- Think about a really small negative number → Positive y value
> - ODD DEGREE
> 	- Think about a really big positive number → positive y value
> 	- Think about a really small negative number → negative y value
> - ! Flip it for negative [[leading coefficient]]




- The end behavior of a [[polynomials\|Polynomial]], whether it’s going up or down at the ends of the equation
- as $x \to \infty, f(x)\to \infty$ is the proper way to say this
	- As x approaches infinity, y approaches infinity
- We can predict the end behavior easily from a [[standard form]] [[polynomials\|polynomial]] based on what is happening to the [[variable math\|variable]] of the first [[term]]
	- [[degree of polynomial\|Degree]], [[Exponents\|exponent]]:
		- If the degree is [[Even]] both ends go in the same direction think abt a [[Parabola]]
		- If the degree is [[odd]] then the ends go in opposite directions → [[Linear Equation]]
	- [[leading coefficient]]:
		- if the [[Coefficient]] is positive it will be like a positive $a$ in [[Vertex Form]] or a positive [[slope]] in the case of a [[Linear Equation]]

		- If the coefficient is negative it will be the opposite
