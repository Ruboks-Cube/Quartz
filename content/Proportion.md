# What is Proportion?

Proportion is when you have equal [[Ratio]]s. 4:5 is proportionate to 8:10, like [[equivalent]] [[Fraction]]s. You multiply both numbers by 2 (or divide by 2) in order to get them, just like how you multiply both the [[Numerator]] and [[Denominator]] by the same number in order to get a fraction that is equal to it.
- In short, Proportion is when you have 2 ratios that are the same ratio just different [[Ratios come in groups\|Groups]]
- There are different types of proportion:
	- [[Direct Proportion]] 
	- [[Inverse Proportion]] 
	- [[joint proportion]]
	- [[Rate]]



## Source Example Problems

### Cookie Recipe

I have a cookie Recipe that calls for 7 cups of flour and 4 cups of sugar. Unfortunately, I only have 3 cups of sugar, so I can't make a full batch. How many cups of flour do I need to make the maximum amount with 3 cups of sugar.

We identify that 7 cups of flour and 4 cups of sugar is a [[Ratio]]! Since this recipe is saying for every 4 cups of sugar, I need 7 cups of flour. So what we need is how much flour for 3 cups of sugar, and keep the ratio Proportional.

#### Using Fraction Equation Explanation
One way to do this is to make a [[Linear Equation]] using [[Fraction]]s. 

$$\frac74=\frac{x}{3}$$
This linear equation is basically asking us what is x if the fractions stay equivalent, and instead of fractions, we can say ratio. [[Using fractions to represent ratios]]

We can use fractions this way because [[Ratio]]s and fractions are quite similar in the way they act. While you do represent [[Ratio]]s with [[Parts to a Whole]] as a fraction to represent the values, **fractions** can also be a way to work with ratios because of their **similarities**.

- they stay **equivalent** meaning 4/5 = 8/10 and 4:5 = 8:10, it's basically just a different sign

> [!NOTE] ❗**IMPORTANT** ❗
> Fractions and Ratios are different, but in order to work with ratios we can use fractions as a substitute, we represent ratios properly with fractions by using parts to a whole


We can start solving the [[Equation]] by multiplying it by 3 in order to get rid of the [[Denominator]] of the right side

$$\frac{21}{4}=x$$
So now we know that x = 21/4. Remember, we are finding out what the amount of flour we need is by using fractions since they are similar to ratios. 

We need 21/4 cups of flour if we have 3 cups of sugar.



#### Using [[Scale Factor]]
Here, we can ask ourselves what we multiply 4 by to get the 3, then [[Multiplication\|Multiply]] that to the 7 as well. This works because since [[Ratio]]s have to stay proportional, when you multiply 1 number by something, you multiply the other(s) by the same thing as well for them to stay proportional.


So, when we ask ourselves what we multiply 4 by to get to 3, we divide 3 by 4 since [[Dividing to find the multiple between 2 numbers\|3 divided by 4 will be what 4 needs to multiply itself by 3 because a/b times b leaves us with a]]
 Since it's 3/4 to multiply 4 to get to 3, we multiply 7 by the same thing.

7 times 3/4 = 21/4, same answer we got in the first solution.
