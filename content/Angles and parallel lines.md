#math/geometry/angles 

## What are transversal lines?
A transversal line is a line that intersects 2 [[parallel\|parallel lines]]. 
like this:
![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240209191411.png]]
The red one is the one that we call a transversal line.


## Why are they special?

Transversal lines are special because of the way their [[Angles]] work.

![[Files/Images/Angles and parallel lines 2024-02-09 19.16.28.excalidraw\|500]]
- You can see here that all the angles here are equal, x and y, we have 2 pairs of [[Vertical Angle]]s due to [[Straight angles\|supplementary angle]]s. But why? How do we know the y on one side is the same as a y on the other? Well it makes sense intuitively, so just trust your gut for now bro, it's literally a copy and paste.
- We call the bottom left angle of the top parallel line and the bottom left of the bottom parallel line [[corresponding angles]]
- ! A way to explain it is that the red line is a straight line with the same [[slope]] when it crosses both [[parallel]] lines the slope stays the same!
