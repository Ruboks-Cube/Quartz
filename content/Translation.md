# Translation
## What is translation
- Suppose that a software program traces a part on a sheet of metal and then produces a copy of the part in another location on the same sheet. Or maybe the program creates multiple copies of a part and places them in a large [[array]].
- Lets see what happens when the [[orientation]] of a [[2D shape\|Shape]] stays the same but the location changes. 

- An Edmunten assignment had me moving around triangles and wanted to see the individual distances of the points. When you moved the triangle a fixed [[Distance]], all the points had moved that same distance. 
- A translation is the displacement of a figure by a specified distance in a specified direction without changing its [[2D shape\|shape]], [[size]], or [[orientation]].
	- A translation can happen easily for even the most complex shapes because we can look at the individual [[point\|points]]. 
		- In this image we can see the [[preimage]] and the [[image (transformation)]]. Each [[point]] in the translated [[image (transformation)]] is the same [[Distance]] from the corresponding point in the [[preimage]] and is shifted in the same direction.  ![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240627082843.png]]
	- If you are given a [[preimage]] then you can find the [[ordered pair\|coordinates]] for any translated [[image (transformation)]]. You just add the [[horizontal]] distance $a$ and the [[vertical]] distance $b$ 
		- Each point $(x,y)$ in the [[preimage]] moves to a point $(x'y')=(x+a,y+b)$ in the [[image (transformation)]]. Note that $a$ and $b$ are [[Real Numbers]]. If $a$ is [[Negative Number\|Negative]] then that means a movement to the left, similarly if $b$ is negative it's a movement down.  
## Translation is [[Rigid Transformations\|rigid]]
- A translation is a [[Rigid Transformations\|Rigid transformation]] 
	- When you translate a shape and make [[Line Segment\|Line segments]] connecting each corresponding point, they are [[parallel]]. 
	- Translation moves points a specified [[Distance]] along a line parallel to a specified line. 
	- Translations are rigid transformations, they preserve the [[Line Segment\|side lengths]] and [[Angles]]. 
	- The [[preimage]] and [[image (transformation)]] are [[congruent]].
