#math/geometry/2d/shape/Triangle 

Find AC
![[Files/Images/Finding Length of Hypotenuse 2024-02-20 18.13.08.excalidraw]]
We have a [[Right Triangle]], and we need to find the hypotenuse, so we can whip out the [[Pythagorean Theorem]].
$$6^2+9^2=117$$

Now we have $c^2$, but we need just $c$.

We can get $c$ by doing $\sqrt{ c^2 }$. In this case $c^2$ is  117, so by finding the [[Radicals and Square Roots\|square root]] of 117 we should get the [[Hypotenuse]]. 
$\sqrt{ 117 }=3\sqrt{ 13 }$
We get $3\sqrt{ 13 }$ by [[Simplifying Square Roots]].
