#math/arithmetic/exponent/radical 
So we know that [[separating and combing radical expressions]] work so we can separate or combine square roots that are being multiplied. What about sums?

There is no "Special Rule" for sums, just know that
$$\sqrt{x}+\sqrt{y},\sqrt{x+y}$$
ARE NOT THE SAME!!!!
