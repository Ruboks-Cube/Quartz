## Simple 1 way transformations
- You can predict transformations before you do them to ensure that they are done properly.
	- You can predict a transformation by mapping [[point\|points]], and trying to visualize what it looks like. ![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240628131946.png]]

## Transformation sequences
- Often you will need more than 1 [[Transformation]] to map one figure on the other
	- Try to first identify what needs to happen to the [[orientation]] of the figure
		- [[Rotation\|Rotations]] and [[Reflection\|Reflections]] will change the orientation
	- [[Translation\|Translations]] are easier since they do not affect the orientation of the figure
- The order of transformations matter
	- [[Rotation\|Rotating]] a figure [[Right Angle\|90 degrees]] CCW then [[Translation\|Translating]] 2 units up does not yield same result as [[Translation\|translating]] 2 units up then rotating [[Right Angle\|90 degrees]]
	- sometimes 1 [[Transformation]] can have the same result as a sequence ![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240628145158.png]]
	- In the diagram you can get $\triangle A''B''C''$ by [[Reflection\|reflecting]] $\triangle ABC$ across the line
	- You can also [[Rotation\|Rotate]] $\triangle ABC$ from [[origin]] by $90^{\circ}$ to get $\triangle A'B'C'$ then [[Reflection\|Reflect]] across $x$ axis to also get $\triangle A''B''C''$
