# How to create a perpendicular line
### Creating a perpendicular bisector
- Place the compass point on one end of the line
- Adjust the compass so it's just half the length of the line 
- Without adjusting the width draw an arc on each side of the line 
	- Diagram:                                                                        ![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240708164933.png]]
- Do the same for the other side and create a line on when the points cross           ![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240708165005.png]]
### Proving it
- $AB$ [[Intersect\|Intersects]] [[Line Segment]] $\overline {PQ}$ from the pairs of intersecting arcs with their endpoints of the given [[Line Segment]] $\overline {PQ}$. Show that $AB$ is indeed [[Perpendicular]] to $PQ$ !
1. Locate the two points where the pairs of arcs intersect and join these points with $P$ and $Q$                                                                                                                 ![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240708171610.png|100]]
2. $AP\cong AQ\cong PB \cong QB$ -> Each of the distances were drawn with the same [[compass]] width
3. $\triangle APQ$ and $\triangle BPQ$ are [[isosceles triangle\|isosceles]]
4. $\angle APC \cong AQC$ -> Base angles of isosceles triangles are [[congruent]] 
5. $\triangle APC \cong \triangle BPQ$ -> [[SSS side side side]]
6. $\angle APC \cong \angle BPC \cong AQC \cong BQC$ -> [[corresponding parts]] of [[congruent]] triangles are [[congruent]]                                                                                                     ![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240708172227.png|100]]
7. $\triangle APB$ and $\triangle AQB$ are [[isosceles triangle\|isosceles]] now prove they're congruent
8. ${} \angle PAC \cong \angle PBC {}$ -> Base angles of isosceles
9. $\triangle APB \cong \triangle AQB$ -> [[SSS side side side]]
10. $\angle PAC \cong \angle QAC \cong \angle PBC \cong \angle QBC$ -> Corresponding parts of congruent triangles 
11. $\triangle APC \cong \triangle BPC \cong \triangle AQC \cong \triangle BQC$ -> [[ASA angle side angle]]
12. $\angle ACP \cong \angle ACQ \cong \angle BCP \cong \angle BCQ$ -> corresponding parts of congruent triangles
13. $AB \perp PQ$ -> 4 angles are equal and add up to 360 $\frac{360}{4}=90$. 
14. $AB$ is the [[perpendicular bisectors\|Perpendicular bisector]] of ${} PQ {}$
