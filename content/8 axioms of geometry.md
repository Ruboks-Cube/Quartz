## The 8 Axioms
- The 8 axioms of geometry are stated in terms of [[point\|points]], [[line\|lines]], [[plane\|Planes]], and [[Distance]] ([[Undefined Term\|undefined terms]])
	1. Given any two points _A_ and _B_, there is exactly one line _AB_ passing through them.
	2. The Distance [[axiom\|Postulate]]: For any two distinct points, there exists a unique positive number called the distance between the two points.
		- [[Distance]] has to be [[Positive Numbers\|positive]] or [[zero, 0\|0]] because it can't be negative.
	3. The Ruler Postulate: The set of points on any line are in one-to-one correspondence with the set of real numbers such that the distance between a pair of points is identified as the absolute value of the difference of the corresponding real numbers.
		- This means every point on a [[Number Line]] corresponds to a [[Real Numbers\|Real number]] and the [[Distance]] is equal to the [[Absolute Value]] of the [[Finding the distance between 2 numbers\|Difference]] between the 2 numbers-- AKA distance is positive
	4. The Ruler Placement Postulate: Given any two points _A_ and _B_ on a line, a coordinate system can be chosen in such a way that the coordinate of _A_ is zero and the coordinate of _B_ is positive. This axiom ensures that any point can be chosen as the origin. That point, together with any other point, can be used to define a positive distance.
		- Any point can be interpreted as [[origin]] depending on the [[Cartesian coordinate system\|coordinate system]]. The point with any other point can be used to define a positive distance. Meaning that 2 [[Negative Number\|Negative numbers]] still have a [[Positive Numbers\|Positive]] distance. Points are relative, distance is relative, the values of the points do not matter if we're talking about the distance, everything is relative.
		- ! Think about it as in, The ruler can be placed anywhere to measure the distance
	1. Every plane contains at least three points that do not lie on the same line. There are at least four points in space that do not lie on the same plane. This axiom distinguishes between [[line\|lines]] (one-dimensional), planes (two-dimensional), and [[space]] (three-dimensional).
		- Every plane needs at least $3$ points, 2 points is just a line, 3 points gives the [[Angles\|angle]] and therefore a plane.
		- Every [[space]] needs at least [[4]] points, you need 4 points for [[3 dimensional]] space since 3 points is just a plane ([[2 dimensional]]) and the 4th point  can be used for height or width, to give [[geometry 3d volume and surface area\|volume]]. 
	2. If two points lie on a plane, then the line containing those points also lies on the plane.
		- This is simple, if 2 points make a [[line]] that line is in the same [[plane]] as the 2 points
	3. Any three points lie on one or more planes. Any three points not on the same line can lie on only one plane.
		- If 3 points are not in a line they make a [[plane]]. Only 1 plane holds all 3 points
	4. The intersection of two planes (if it exists) is a line. ![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240626190957.png]]
