#math/algebra/matrix 

- The inverse matrix is what you multiply by 1 [[matrix]] to get the [[Identity Matrix]] 
- How do you find the inverse matrix?
- For 2x2 matrices, here’s how:
	1. Take original matrix $\begin{bmatrix}a&b \\c&d\end{bmatrix}$
	2. find determinant of that matrix
	3. get the [[Reciprocal]] of the determinant and multiply by the [[adjoint]] 

- Sometimes, the matrix HAS no inverse
	- We call these matrices singular
	- This happens when the [[determinant]] is 0


- ! Very important to remember its the reciprocal of the [[determinant]]
