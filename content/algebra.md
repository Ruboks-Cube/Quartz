### 7. Proportion
- 7.1, [[Direct Proportion]]
	- [[Proportion\|What is proportion]]?
	- [[Direct Proportion]] 
- 7.2, [[Inverse Proportion]]
- 7.3, [[joint proportion]]
	- [[joint proportion#Key Ideas\|Key ideas in Joint Proportion]]
- 7.4 [[Rate\|Rate Problems]]
- Key ideas: 
	- ## Key Ideas
- If $x$ and $y$ are directly proportional, that means the [[Fraction\|Quotient]] of $\frac{x}{y}$ is constant. In other words, $\frac{x}{y}=k$ always. Another way to say this is that $x$ is a multiple of $y$ always, we have $x=ky$
- [[Direct Proportion]] problems are similar to normal [[Ratio]] problems, a <mark class="hltr-yellow">key step in solving them is to find the constant ratio of quantities</mark>. The Constant ratio of quantities is the <mark class="hltr-green">smallest group basically, a simplified version of the ratio</mark>, a [[Simplifying Fractions\|Simplified Fraction]]. In [[Direct Proportion#7.1\|Problem 7.1]], the <mark class="hltr-red">constant ratio of quantities</mark> for (a) was $9:5$. It can sometimes be referred to as Ratio of Proportionality, or constant of proportionality, and later, [[slope]].
- We can find the constant of proportionality to solve a **direct proportions** problem, or we can find how many [[Ratios come in groups\|Groups of the ratio]] there is by comparing like quantities like we did in solution 2 of [[Direct Proportion#7.2\|Problem 7.2]]. Both work, and both are important to understand.
	- ## Key Ideas
- If $x$ and $y$ are inversely proportional then the product $xy$ is constant. We can write this as $xy=k$. Sometimes, $k$ is referred to as the constant of proportionality.
- As $x$ goes up, $y$ comes down, and vice versa. When we multiply $x$ by something, we multiply $y$ by the [[Reciprocal]] of what we multiplied $x$ by.
	- ## Key Ideas
- Joint Proportion is when we have one relationship between 2 variables, and another with the same variable from the previous with another variable, and we find an [[Expression]] or [[equations\|Equation]] on how they are ALL related.

> [!WARNING] IMPORTANT
> We say a variable like $x$ is in jointly proportional to a group of other variables if $x$ is directly proportional to each of those variables. If all other variables are constant, like $x$ is jointly proportional to $y$ and $z$ we can write $x=kyz$ for where $k$ is a constant. We can write this as $\frac{x}{yz}=k$, showing that $x$ and $yz$ are directly proportional, and so is $\frac{x}{y}$ and $\frac{x}{z}$ to $y$ or $z$ on the other side.
> - JOINT PROPORTION IS WHEN ONE VARIABLE IS DIRECTLY PROPORTIONAL TO A PRODUCT OF OTHER VARIABLES
- A nice way to find joint proportions is to group the varying quantities ([[variable math\|variables]]) on one side of the equation so we're left with constants on the other, leaving us <mark class="hltr-yellow">a direct proportion.</mark>
- When we have joint proportion, like $\frac{x}{yz}$, $x$ is directly proportional to $yz$, and if the product of $yz$ gets bigger because like $y$ got doubled, or $z$ got doubled, then $x$ will increase by the same AMOUNT.
- If $x$ is [[Direct Proportion\|Directly Proportional]] to $y$, then we have $\frac{x}{y}=k$. If $x$ is in [[Direct Proportion]] to $z$, we have $\frac{x}{z}=k$. $x$ is directly proportional to both $y$ and $z$. If $z$ stays constant, we have $\frac{x}{y}=k*z$. If $y$ stays constant, we have $\frac{x}{z}=k*y$. (Ratio of $\frac{x}{z}*$ how many $y
	- ## Key Ideas
- Rate and Time are [[Inverse Proportion\|Inversely Proportional]] to Distance if Distance stays constant.
- Make sure to keep careful track of your units
- [[RT=D Rate times Time equals Distance]] can be applied to anything that has work per unit of time. Rate of work $*$ Time worked $=$ amount of work done.
- If two objects are moving in a problem, sometimes it's easier to consider how the objects are moving relative to each other than to consider them separately like we did in solution 2 of [[Rate#7.13\|Problem 7.13]].
- You have to decide whether you add or multiply in these problems carefully. Make sure it makes sense in the context of the problem
- Really really make sure that you [[variables as unknown values\|Set variables for unknown values!]]
- Rate times Time equals distance, if you are asked for 1 value, see if you can find the other 2 values. 
- The rate is just a simplified [[Ratio]] between amount of work done and time.
- Problem solving strategies
	- there is not a single "right way" to do a problem
	- [[Trying a few basic ideas is a great way to explore a new type of problem]]
	- Many problems involving any type of work can be solved
