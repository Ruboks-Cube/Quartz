#math/geometry #math/geometry/2d/shape #math/geometry/2d 

- When we have a [[2D shape\|shape]] and we cut it into 2 identical pieces we are using a [[line]] of symmetry.
	- A line of symmetry is a line that splits the shape in 2, leaving 2 shapes that are identical.
	- If a [[line of reflection]] is a line of symmetry, nothing about the shape changes.


- There is a difference with a line of symmetry from a [[Even]]-sided [[Polygon]] and a [[odd]]-sided polygon. 
	- If the [[Polygon]] has an even number of sides the line of symmetry can be through opposite [[vertex\|vertices]] and be [[perpendicular bisectors]] of opposite sides. (split the sides)  ![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240627153308.png|300]]
	- For an Odd sided [[Polygon]] they go from [[vertex]] to [[Perpendicular]] bisector of a [[Line Segment\|side length]].                                                                      ![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240627153639.png|300]]
