#math #english/definition 


- Theorems are statements that are proven to be true by a logical sequence of statements that make the use of [[axiom\|axioms]], [[Definition\|Definitions]], [[Undefined Term\|Undefined Terms]] and other Theorems. 
- The logical, or intuitive deduction of a theorem is called a [[proof]]. 
	- Once a theorem is proven to be true it can be used to in the proof of new facts. 
	- This process, where everything builds on everything else is called an [[axiomatic system\|Axiomatic Approach]].
