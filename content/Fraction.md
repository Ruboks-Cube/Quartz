#math/arithmetic #math/arithmetic/divisionmultiplication #math/arithmetic/divisionmultiplication/fraction #tag 



A fraction in math is a part of something. It's another way to show [[Division]], as a part of something multiplied to a specific number will equal a whole of something else.

Fractions have a [[Numerator]] and a [[Denominator]]. 


See also: [[Simplifying Fractions]]
### Examples:

$$\frac14$$
1 being the numerator, 4 being the denominator. Something (in this case 1 whole) was split into 4 pieces, and you have 1 of them.

What do I mean by 1 whole? 

In most cases it will be 1 whole, but a lot of the times it can be more. Think about having 1 pizza, split into 4 pieces, 3 of your friends already ate a slice and now there is 1 left.





### Multiplying a fraction by the denominator

If you multiply the fraction by the [[Denominator]] only, you get the [[Numerator]]. This is important when [[Dealing With Fractions in Equations-Inequalities]].
