## How to inscribe a square

- We start off with drawing the [[diameter]] of the circle and then drawing the [[perpendicular bisectors\|perpendicular bisector]]
	- CONNECTIONS -> [[bisector]], [[Perpendicular]]
	- Then connect the points from where the lines intersect
![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240708220337.png|300]]

- Why this works:
	![[Files/Images/inscribing shapes in circles 2024-07-08 22.05.03.excalidraw\|300]]
	- There are 2 main parts of a [[Perfect Squares\|Square]]
		- All [[Line Segment\|side lengths]] are equal
		- All [[Angles]] are equal
	- ? So all we have to do is prove that all angles and sides are equal right???
	- This is really easy to do!
		- Sides:
			- We know that all the points from our "square" are equidistant (AKA equal to each other) because the 2 [[diameter\|Diameters]]--[[bisector\|bisect]] each other 
		- [[Angles]]
			- Like the picture above shows, the 4 triangles made from the bisecting diameters create [[isosceles triangle\|isosceles]] triangles that are all [[congruent]] through [[SAS side angle side]]
			- We know that the 2 angles that are not 90 degrees are [[congruent]] and the only way this can happen is if they are $45^{\circ}$
			- All the [[Triangle\|Triangles]] are congruent so the angles are [[Complementary Angles\|complementary]] showing that it's indeed a square
