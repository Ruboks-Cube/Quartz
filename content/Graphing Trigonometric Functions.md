#math/geometry/2d/shape/Triangle/trigonometry #math/function #math/geometry/2d/shape/Triangle 


- How would we graph $\sin \theta$? What about $\cos \theta$?
- These are derived from [[The Unit Circle]] → [[The unit circle is not a function]]
- In a graph, the [[Angles\|angle]] is the $x$ axis (when we make the graph they will be in [[radian\|radians]]) 
	- The value of the [[function]] will be the $y$ [[axis]]
- Use [[period\|periods]] to say “full revolution” (graph with 1-360 degrees)
- In these functions $\theta=x$ 
- For these functions, the [[parent function]] is $y=\sin \theta$ or $y=\cos \theta$
	- Differ from the parent by altering the values of $a,b,c,d$ in:
		$y=a\sin (b\theta-c)+d$ 
- [[swapping from sin to cosine and vice versa]]
# How to actually plot it
- We need to plot 5 points to graph a [[period]] 
> [!help] Why 5 points
> We need to think about the [[quadrantal]] [[Angles]], there are $5$ (0 and 360 count as 2)
> The period either smushes the graph or expands it, but we always plot in relation to the quadrantal angles.
- Start with [[identifying the period]]
- Then after the period, you split it into 4ths from $0$ to the period
	- (easily done by multiplying the period by 1/4 and then keep adding till you GET to the period??)
	- you have your $x$ axis!!
- [[Frame shift]]
- Then find the [[midline]] and [[amplitude]] points
	- Is there a [[reflection across x axis for trigonometric functions\|Reflection across the x axis]]??
- Then you graph, depending on whether its [[graphing sine function]] or [[graphing cosine function]]
- [[Graphing tan function]] and [[graphing cotan function]] is completely different
- [[Graphing Secant Function]] and [[Graphing Cosecant Function]] stays related
