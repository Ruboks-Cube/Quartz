#math/geometry #math/geometry/3d #math/geometry/2d/shape #math/geometry/2d , [[2 dimensional]], [[3 dimensional]], [[3D shape]]


- A cross section is when you pass a [[2 dimensional]]--[[plane]] through a [[3D shape]]. 
	- The cross section takes the space/[[Area]] where the [[plane]] and [[3D shape\|shape]] [[Intersect]].
- The [[2D shape\|shape]] of a cross section depends on the [[Angles\|angle]] of the [[plane]] slicing through the shape.
	- [[what it means when someone says horizontal or vertical in context of cross sections]]
- When the number of faces increase the number of sides for the cross section will also increase for the different positions. This is because there is a wider range of faces to cut through.
	- ! The number of sides the cross section can have depends on the number of faces the [[3D shape]] has. 
		- If the 3D shape has 5 faces, the plane only has the ability to have 5 sides since the 3D shape and the plane can only intersect at 5 points.
