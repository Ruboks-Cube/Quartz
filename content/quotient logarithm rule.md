#math/arithmetic/logarithm 

- The quotient logarithm rule is like the [[product logarithm rule]], but its for when the logarithms are being subtracted
	- It’s basically $\log_{b}a-\log_{b}c=\log_{b}{\frac{a}{c}}$
- PROOF
	- $\log_{b}a=x$
	- $\log_{b}c=y$ 
	- $x-y=\log_{b}a-\log_{b}c$
	- $b^x=a$
	- $b^y=c$
	- $\frac{b^x}{b^y}=\frac{a}{c}$ 
	- $\frac{b^x}{b^y}=b^{x-y}$ → [[Same Base exponent rules]]
	- $b^{x-y}=\frac{a}{c}$
	- SO $\log_{b}{\frac{a}{c}}=x-y$ 
	- [[substitution]] gives us $\log_{b}{\frac{a}{c}=\log_{b}a-\log_{b}c}$
