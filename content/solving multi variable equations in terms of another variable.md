#math/algebra/inequality/equations , [[equations\|Equation]], [[multi variable expressions]]
## Introduction to multi variable equations
- Equations can have many variables too
	- Take this equation $x+2y=3$
	- How can we solve this? If we're solving for $x$, and $y$ was just a normal number, we'd just isolate $x$ through [[Inverse Operation]] since it's a normal [[One variable linear equation]]
	- This time, we have 2 variables, but we can still isolate $x$
	- Subtract $2y$ from both sides to get $x=3-2y$
	- We just isolated $x$, and now we know that $x$ is $3-2y$
	- We just solved for $x$ in "terms" or $y$, meaning if the $y$ value changes the value of $x$ changes as well

### 4.16, combining multiplication and addition
![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240425082822.png]]
- Subtract $b$ from both sides to get $ax=c-b$ 
- divide both sides by $a$ to get $x=\frac{{c-b}}{a}$
### 4.17, a real challenge
![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240425083015.png]]
- We have a lot of variables and terms in this one. We need to move all the variables to the right that are NOT $x$ and move $x$ to the left
- We have $ax+2d^2x=3c-bc$
- How will we get rid of $a$ and $2d^2$?
- We can factor out $x$ on the left side to get $x(a+2d^2)=3c-bc$
- Divide both sides by $a+2d^2$ to get $x=\frac{{3c-bc}}{a+2d^2}$
