#math/geometry/creating [[Angles]], [[bisector]], 


# how to
- Set the [[compass]] to any convenient width and draw an [[arc]] across each leg. ![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240708163211.png|200]]
- From where the arc crosses the leg make an arc in the angles interior, the compass can be adjusted if desired at this point but the same width must be needed for both legs                                                                                            ![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240708163342.png|200]]
- Draw a line from $Q$ to where the arcs cross                                                                ![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240708163417.png]]
- We've bisected the angle

# Why it works
- Bisecting  an angle could be done by creating a [[Reflection]] of the angle directly opposite to it and then drawing a straight line from one point to the other, splitting the shape and therefore the angle
- The first 2 arcs are equidistant from Q
	- Taking the [[Finding the midpoint between 2 points\|midpoint]] would bisect Q
		- [[midpoint]]
	- This is kind of the first two steps of finding the midpoint lowk
- We draw two arcs that both [[Intersect]] at a point [[equidistant]] from the first 2 arcs
	- Since it’s equidistant that means drawing two lines from the first two arcs creates a perfect reflection of angle Q because the first 2 arcs are also equidistant from point Q
- Drawing a line from Q to where the 2nd 2 arcs intersect simply splits the visualized shape
