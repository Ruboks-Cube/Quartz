#math/algebra/inequality/equations/polynomial #math/arithmetic/divisionmultiplication 

- If the [[remainder]] of [[dividing polynomials]] is 0 then the [[Divisor]] is a [[Factor]]/[[root]] of the [[Dividend]]
- If the remainder isn’t 0 you still have a [[ordered pair]]
	- ? Why?
		- You have the [[Factor]] of the polynomial $(x+a)$ where $(-a)$ is the $x$ value of the [[point]]
		- Then you have the remainder $r$, where $(x+a)$ [[Multiplication\|multiplied]] by the [[Dividend]] PLUS $\frac{r}{(x+a)}$ equals the original [[polynomials\|polynomial]]
			- So we have this structure: $(x+a)(\text{dividend}+\frac{r}{(x+a)})$
			- Distributing gives us $(x+a)\text{dividend}+(x+a)\left( \frac{r}{x+a} \right)$ 
			- This leaves us with $(x+a)\text{dividend}+r$ and when $x=-a$ we’re left with $r=y$
