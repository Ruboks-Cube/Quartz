# Same base quotient of powers
## Explaining
What if you have a case like $$\frac{x^{n}}{x^{n+y}}$$
For example,

$$\frac{4^8}{4^4}$$


What you do here is you find the difference of the exponents, and keep the base.

$$\frac{4^8}{4^4}=\frac{4\cdot4\cdot4\cdot4\cdot4\cdot4\cdot4\cdot4}{4\cdot4\cdot4\cdot4}$$
The 4's that are being multiplied cancel out and become 1's, eventually you will be left with 4 4's at the top and only 1 on the bottom, making $$4^4$$
So you take the difference. This can lead to [[Negative Exponents]].
## Some problems
![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240413092203.png]]
- (a) $\frac{x^7}{x^3}$ = $x^4$ because $7-3=4$
- (b) $\frac{2x}{6}=\frac{x}{3}$ because of [[Simplifying Fractions]] and [[cancel out\|Cancelling out things]]
- (c) $\frac{{6x^2}}{x}=6x$
- (d) $\frac{{4x^9}}{20x^2}=\frac{{x^7}}{5}$
