### Proving it by [[writing proofs\|Writing a proof]]
- We let [[point\|points]] $A$, $B$, $C$ make a [[Triangle]] -> Given
- We let $\overleftrightarrow{DE}$ be a line passing through $B$, parallel to $\overline{AC}$ as shown:                                 ![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240706083510.png]]
-   $1\cong 4$ and $3 \cong 5$ [[Angles and parallel lines\|Alternate Interior Angles Theorem]]
- This means $1=4$ and $3=5$ -> definition of [[congruent]]
- $\angle 4+\angle 2+\angle 5=180^{\circ}$ -> Angle addition and [[Straight angles]]
- $\angle 1+\angle 2+\angle 3=180^{\circ}$ -> [[substitution property of equality]]
