# How you write logarithms
- The $\log_{b}x$ just means $b$ to the power of what will equal $x$?
- This means $b^y=x$ where you’re like SOLVING for $y$ 
	- This also goes in reverse, since $b^y=x$ then $\log_{b}x=y$ 
	- In this case, $b$ is the [[base]] (think about [[Exponents]]) while $x$ is the [[argument log\|argument]]
