#math/function/exponential 

- Euler’s number important in [[Continuous Compound Interest]]
	- Lets say you have a money 100$ you put in bank with [[interest rate]] 6%
	- So we have $y=100\left( 1+\frac{0.06}{r} \right)^{rt}$
	- As $r$ gets bigger, the base gets closer to Eulers Number, 2.71
	- You can see this w/ desmos if you plug in the equation $y=100\left( 1+\frac{1}{x} \right)^x$ it gets REALLY close to $e$  (put 1), $e$ is the *base* for [[Continuous Compound Interest]] interest as it represents the max increase we can get from increasing the [[Rate]]
	 ![[Files/Pasted image 20250324192242.png]]
