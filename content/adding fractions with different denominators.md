To add fractions with different [[Denominator]], you first have to find a common denominator. Everything else kind of falls into place and you add [[Fraction\|fractions]] normally.


To find the common denominator you have to multiply both denominators together.

$\frac{4}{15}+\frac{3}{4}$, $15*4=60$, $60$ will the be common denominator. But it's not going to be $\frac{4}{60}+\frac{3}{60}$, we have to change the [[Numerator\|Numerators]] as well. 

We are essentially making an [[equivalent]] fraction, we multiply 15 by 4 so we also multiply 4 by 4, to get the fraction $\frac{16}{60}$ and the same thing to the other fraction $\frac{3}{4}$, we multiply 4 by 15 so we multiply 3 by 15 as well to get $\frac{45}{60}$. 

Now our [[Expression]] is $\frac{16}{60}+\frac{45}{60}=\frac{61}{60}$, what a terrible fraction.
