### what is pi

#math/geometry/2d/shape/circle 
Circumference divided by diameter equals pi, always equals pi.
## explanation

The [[Ratio]] between the [[circumference]] and the [[diameter]] always stays the same, no matter how big the circle is. This makes sense because you are changing the whole circle by the same 
scale factor, since all circles are [[similar]], the ratio will always be the same.

But when you look at what the ratio is, it is 3.14. When you divide the circumference by the diameter, we get 3.14. This is called pi, or: $\pi$ 
Pi is a [[decimal]] that goes forever, it's not repeating, but it goes on forever, similar to the [[Square root of 2]], it is an [[Irrational Number]].

## What is the [[Area]] of a circle?
### Explanation
How do we find the area of a circle?

If we cut a circle into multiple pieces like this:
![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240219191128.png|200]]
Then rearrange them like this:
![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240219191150.png]]
It almost looks like a [[quadrilateral]]

Moving 1 triangle to the other side makes this:
![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240219191227.png]]
So it ALMOSTS looks like a [[Rectangle]]. ALMOST
But what are the 2 side lengths?

Well, the width would be the radius of the circle, and the length would be 1/2 the circumference, because the 2 lengths make up 1 circumference.

So we multiply the radius by 1/2 the circumference, the circumference is just the diameter times pi, 1/2 the circumference is the radius times pi, so we get
$$\pi r^2$$
And we're done
