#math #math/algebra [[math]], [[algebra]], [[equations\|Equation]] 


In math, we have different operations called [[arithmetic]]. They are [[addition]], [[subtraction]], [[Multiplication]], [[Division]], and [[Exponents]]. 

> [!warning] Dividing by 0
> You are unable to divide by 0, another rule in math.


While each operation itself is simple, we need rules to know which order to do the operations in certain [[Expression\|Expressions]].

For example:
$2+3*4$, does this expression mean to do $2+3$ first or $3*4$, both lead to different answers?


> [!important] The way to do it in order is
> **p**-arenthesis. Do anything thats inside () in an expression first
> **e**-xponents. Solve [[Exponents]] in an expression next.
> **m**-ultiplication. Solve multiplication next.
> **d**-ivision. Solve division next
> **a**-ddition. Solve addition next
> **s**-ubtraction. Solve Subtraction next
>>[!note] The operations that are opposite, do them left to right
>>For the operations multiplication and division, if you are left with multiplication and division you can do them left to right because they are basically the same thing, [[Why is division the same as multiplying by reciprocal\|Division is same as multipliying by reciprocal]].
>>For addition and subtraction, subtraction is just addition with [[Negative Number\|Negative Numbers]]
>
>>[!warning] subtraction and division are not Commutative
>>subtraction and division do not have [[Commutative and Associative Property\|Commutative Property]], meaning you can't do them in any order (unless you think of them as their opposites.)
>>For example, $5-2=3$, and $2-5=-3$ so you can see they are not the same, but you can change $2-5=2+(-5)=-5+2$ since we think of minus 5 as adding -5
>>do you see the difference?!
