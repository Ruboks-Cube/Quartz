#math/algebra/inequality/equations/Ratios_Proportions_Rates 

The whole of a group is the full value of a ratio.


## Examples
In the ratio 4:8, there are two values, 4 to 8. It states that for every 4 things, there's 8 other things.

The total amount of things is 12, and in that 12 there is 4 and 8.

Lets say we have some apples, for every 4 green apples, there are 8 red ones. (4:8)
We can represent this group as a diagram (remember, group just refers to the values of the ratio, the above statement also says in a group of apples, 4 are green, 8 are red)
