#math #math/algebra #math/function/exponential 

- Compound Interest is an [[economy]] ahh term
	- It’s expressed with [[exponential function\|exponential functions]]
	- You’re given a time period WHEN The formula will increase your money in an account
		- When that time period hits the formula multiplies that amount of money by 100% + the % increase
	- The rate of an increase is called an [[interest rate]]
- The “compound” part is a [[compound period]]
	- It means that every single “compound interest” has an [[interest rate]] and is tied to one year
	- A simple example would be ${} y=a(1+r)^x {}$ where $x$ represents one year, $y$ is money, $a$ is the starting money, and $r$ is the [[interest rate]] (convert percentage to fraction)
	- What abt quarterly interest? and stuff
		- These would be $y=a\left( 1+\frac{r}{4} \right)^{4x}$ where $x$ is again 1 year
		- This  multiplies the exponent by 4 because it happens 4 times a year, but we want the rate to be almost the same so we divide it by 4 since we’re doing it 4 times a year
