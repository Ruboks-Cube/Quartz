#math/statistics 

The median or middle is the middle of all the data points. To get the median you list all the values from least to greatest, and see which one is directly in the middle.

Finding the median is different if you have an odd or even number amount of values.
For an [[odd]] amount of values, lets say x, y, and z, where $x<y<z$ is true, the median would just be y, because y is in the middle. 

for an [[Even]] number, lets say x, y, z, w, where we have $x<y<z<w$, the median would be the average of the two middle numbers. So we would do $\frac{{y+z}}{2}$ to get the median of an Even number, you could also say thats just the midpoint between $y$ and $z$.



### Number in the middle, Even

1,2,3...54,55,56

What is the median?

Ok, so we know that there is 56 numbers in the list. 56 is an even number, which means half of it + another half is going to be 56. This means that $\frac{56}{2}$ (28) is going to be one half, and 28.5 is going to be the middle since 28 is half on both sides, 56 is made up of 2 28's, so the middle of 56 would be 28.5


### Number in the middle, odd

1,2,3...53,55

What is the median?

First lets consider a problem like 1,2,3

In this one we can easily see that the median is 2, which is 3/2 +0.5. This sticks with what happened in the Even number thing, where the median was 56/2+0.5

This shows that the median of any number is going to be the number divided by 2.




Another way to think about it, is that the list goes from 1-55 in this specific problem. We can sort of erase all the other numbers and be left with 1 and 55, then find the median of these two which is again, 28.
