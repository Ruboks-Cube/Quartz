#Coding 

- Used to assign values to [[variable in coding\|variables]] 
	- $=$ is an assignment operator

- There are many assignment operators:
	- All you need to know is there is a **modifier** before the $=$ sign to change what it means
	- E.G, if there’s a $x*=3$, it’s the same as $x=x*3$, so you can think of it as $x*3$ and get rid of the $=$ sign
		- You may recognize these from [[arithmetic operators]]
- Here are examples of different modifiers
	- $+$, $-$, $*$. $/$, % ([[Modulus]]), ^ ([[Exponents]])
