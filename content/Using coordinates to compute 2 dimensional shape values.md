#math/geometry #math/geometry/2d #math/graphing , [[ordered pair\|coordinates]], [[Geometry]], [[2 dimensional shape values]], [[2D shape]], [[2 dimensional]], [[Perimeter]], [[Area]]

### Perimeter
- You calculate the [[Perimeter]] by finding the sum of all the [[Line Segment\|side lengths]] 
- If you know the [[ordered pair\|coordinates]] of the [[vertex\|vertices]] you can calculate the [[Line Segment\|side lengths]] with using the [[Distance formula]]/[[Pythagorean Theorem]]

### Area
- You can also use [[ordered pair\|coordinates]] to find the [[Area]] of a shape
	- To find the [[Area]] of a [[Triangle]] you have to have the height and base.
		- To get the height you need to find the length of the [[Line Segment]] connecting a [[vertex]] and the base. This line has to be [[Perpendicular]]. 
		- To find the base find the distance between the two [[endpoints]]
		- Once you have the height and base, use the formula: $\frac{\text{base*height}}{2}$ 
	- To find the area of a [[Rectangle]] you have to have 2 [[adjacent]] side lengths
		- Find 2 adjacent (non-[[congruent]] unless it's a [[Perfect Squares\|Square]]) [[Line Segment\|side lengths]] and multiply to find the area
