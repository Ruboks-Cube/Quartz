#math/geometry/2d/shape/circle , [[Circle]], [[Chord]], [[Intersect]]

- When 2 [[Chord\|chords]] share an [[endpoints\|endpoint]] they create an [[inscribed angle]]
	- there are times when 2 chords do not just intersect at an endpoint which creates a special situation
		- If 2 [[Chord\|chords]]-[[Intersect]] the [[Line Segment\|line segments]] into which they are split have related lengths. [[Intersect\|intersecting]]-[[Chord\|chords]] also form [[Angles\|angles]] whose measures are related to the measures of [[arc\|arcs]] defined by their [[Intersect\|intersection]] 
			![[Files/Pasted image 20240802203012.png|Diagram|200]]
			- When 2 chords intersect, the product of the lengths of the [[Line Segment\|segments]] into which each chord is divided is the same.
				- Look at figure 1. $AE*EC=BE*ED$
				- This also means that $\frac{AE}{BE}=\frac{ED}{EC}$  -> the lengths of the corresponding line segments are in the same [[Ratio]] 
			- When 2 chords [[Intersect]] the measure of an [[Angles\|angle]] between them is equal to half the sum of the measure of the [[Intersect\|intersected]]-[[arc\|arcs]] for that angle and the angle vertically opposed to it
			- Look at figure 2
				- We have an angle $FJI$ which [[Intersect\|intersects]] $\overparen{FI}$ and $\overparen{GH}$. $\angle FJI=\frac{1}{2}(\overparen{GH}+\overparen{IF})$ 
				- Like [[Angles\|angle]] $FJI$ which corresponds to [[arc]] $IF$ also has a vertical angle $GHJ$ that corresponds to arc $GH$. Arc $GH+IF$ divided by 2 equals ${} FJI {}$
