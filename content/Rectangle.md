#math/geometry/2d/shape 
A rectangle is like a [[Perfect Squares\|Square]]. It's a 2 dimensional [[Polygon]] that has 4 sides and each [[vertex]] has a [[Right Angle]].

The [[Area]] of a rectangle is the length, times the width. [[Length and Width]]
![[Files/Images/Rectangle 2024-02-13 20.55.23.excalidraw]]
It makes sense because we start off with the width, then multiply that by the length, you can imagine sliding the width to get the area.

## If a [[quadrilateral]] is a rectangle then its [[diagonals]] are congruent
- Given that [[Rectangle diagonal ABCD.png|Quadrilateral ABCD]] is a rectangle. Prove diagonals $\overline {AC}$ and $\overline {BD}$ are [[congruent]]. 
	1. Quadrilateral ABCD is a rectangle -> Given 
		- Diagram:                                                             ![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240708105045.png]]
	2. Rectangle ABCD is a [[parallelogram]] -> Definition of rectangle
	3. $\overline {AB}\cong \overline {CD}$, and $\overline{BC}\cong \overline {AD}$ -> Opposite sides of [[parallelogram\|Parallelograms]] are [[congruent]]
	4. $\angle ABC =\angle DCB$ -> Definition of rectangles
	5. $\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DCB$ -> [[SAS side angle side]] 
	6. $\overline {AC}\cong \overline {BD}$ -> [[corresponding parts]] of [[Triangle\|Triangles]] are congruent.
