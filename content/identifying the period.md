#math/function #math/geometry/2d/shape/Triangle/trigonometry 

- To identify the [[period]] in [[Trigonometry\|Trigonometric Functions]] we pay attention to values affecting $\theta$/$x$
- These can be affected in two ways

## multiplying
> [!note] Multiplying something by $\theta$
> E.G. Like doing $y=\sin(2\theta)$
> 2 is being multiplied by $\theta$ and that changes the [[period]]
> > [!important]
> The default [[period]]/full revolution is $2\pi$
> 
> If we multiply something by $\theta$, its making the $x$ values happen *twice as fast*, so it would SMUSH $x$ 2 times.
> This means the period would be $\frac{2\pi}{2}$ in $\sin(2\theta)$
