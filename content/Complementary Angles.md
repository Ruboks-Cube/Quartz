#math/geometry/angles 

Complementary angles are similar to [[Straight angles\|supplementary angle]]s, but they add up to a different value.

Complementary angles are angles that add up to 90 degrees, so if we have a [[Right Triangle]], since one of the angles are already [[Right Angle\|a Right Angle]], and since the sum of
a triangles angles have to be 180 degrees, we know that the other 2 angles are complementary, since their sum must be 90 degrees.
