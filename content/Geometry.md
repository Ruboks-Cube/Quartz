> - When 2 [[Chord\|chords]] intersect, the measure of an [[Angles\|angle]] between them is equal to half the sum of the [[arc\|arcs]] intercepted by the angle AND the angle [[vertical]] to it. ([[Vertical Angle]])

- Relating [[arc]] length and [[Area]] to the [[radius]]
	- [[arc length]]
	- [[radian]]
		- [[converting between Radians and Degrees]] 
	- Area of a [[sector]] 
 > [!summary]
> - The length of an arc is: ${} \frac{\text{measure of central angle}}{360^{\circ}}*2\pi r {}$ ($2\pi r$ is [[circumference]] of circle) -> 
> 	- The length of an arc is a [[Fraction]] of the whole circumference which is the same fraction as the central angle to $360^{\circ}\to \text{angle of whole circle}$
> - The [[Area]] of a [[sector]] is $\frac{\text{measure of central angle}}{360^{\circ}}*\pi r^2$ ($\pi r^2$ is the area of the whole circle)
> 	- The area of a [[sector]] is a [[Fraction]] of the whole circles area which is the same fraction as the $\frac{\text{measure of central angle}}{360^{\circ}}$ js like the length of an arc
> - A [[radian]] is js another way to measure an [[arc]] or [[Angles\|angle]] that shows a relationship between the [[arc length]] and the [[radius]]
> 	- $\theta(\text{Radians})=\frac{\text{length of arc}}{\text{radius}}$
> 	- $\theta=\frac{\text{angle measure}}{180}*\pi$ -> For any angle measure because $360^{\circ}=2\pi \text{ Radians}$
