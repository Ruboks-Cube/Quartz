#math/algebra/inequality/equations/Ratios_Proportions_Rates 

- A rate is a [[Ratio]] with 2 different units. Sometimes the unit has to do with time.

- Coffees drank per day, Miles per hour, pencils sharpened per period (45 minutes), they are all ratios, coffee to day, miles to hours, pencils sharpened to 45 minutes.
- Rates are an example of [[joint proportion]]. 

# Rate Problems
## Key Ideas
- Rate and Time are [[Inverse Proportion\|Inversely Proportional]] to Distance if Distance stays constant.
- Make sure to keep careful track of your units
- [[RT=D Rate times Time equals Distance]] can be applied to anything that has work per unit of time. Rate of work $*$ Time worked $=$ amount of work done.
- If two objects are moving in a problem, sometimes it's easier to consider how the objects are moving relative to each other than to consider them separately like we did in solution 2 of [[Rate#7.13\|Problem 7.13]].
- You have to decide whether you add or multiply in these problems carefully. Make sure it makes sense in the context of the problem
- Really really make sure that you [[variables as unknown values\|Set variables for unknown values!]]
- Rate times Time equals distance, if you are asked for 1 value, see if you can find the other 2 values. 
- The rate is just a simplified [[Ratio]] between amount of work done and time.

- Solution 1 *Examine each page separately*
	- When Carl has started reading, Flow has already read for 30 minutes, so she's read 30 pages already. Let $m$ be the number of minutes after 1:30 that the two have been reading for. Flow has been reading for $30$ minutes and she reads a page every minute, so the amount of pages she's read $m$ minutes after 1:30 is $m+30$. Carl reads a page every $50$ seconds, so he reads 1 page every $\frac{5}{6}$ of a minute. Since Carl reads a page every $\frac{5}{6}$ of a minute, his reading rate is $\frac{1page}{\frac{5}{6}min}=\frac{6}{5}$ pages per minute. (when we had a page every 50 seconds, we want his rate for every minute since that's what $m$ is) We can also explain why we divide with [[Ratio\|ratios]]
	- After $m$ minutes, Carl has read $\frac{6m}{5}$ pages. If the two have read the same number of pages after $m$ minutes, we have $m+30=\frac{6m}{5}$. Solving this we get $m=150$, so carl catches up $150$ minutes after he starts, at $4:00$. 
- Solution 2: *Consider Relative Values*
	- Carl reads $\frac{6}{5}$ of a page per minute while Flo reads $1$ page per minute. This means that he will catch up by $\frac{1}{5}$ a page per minute because we have $\frac{6}{5}-1=\frac{1}{5}$ page per minute. This makes sense because once Flo has read $1$ page, Carl has read $\frac{6}{5}$ of $1$ page, we subtract Carl's from Flo's to get $\frac{1}{5}$ page per minute because that's how much he's caught up.
	- Flo has read $30$ pages already, and Carl catches up $\frac{1}{5}$ page per minute. $\frac{30page}{\frac{1}{5} \frac{page}{min}}=150$ minutes. We divide because it's like he's reading $\frac{1}{5}$ a page per minute in this situation and he has to read $30$ pages to catch up to Flo!
		- Think of it as Flo reads 1 page, Carl also reads 1 page, so those two cancel out, but then Carl also reads $\frac{1}{5}$ of a page, and since Flo has already read 30 pages he has to read that many pages with a rate of $\frac{1}{5}$
- In this problem, both Flo and Carl are "moving" through the book, like the book is a road of words and they're walking through it, each intersection representing a page turn and when you get to a different section of the city it represents a different chapter.
