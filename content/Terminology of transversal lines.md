## Terminology of transversal lines
- Here is an image of a transversal line:         ![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240703205533.png]]
- On top of [[corresponding angles]] there is more to [[Angles and parallel lines\|transversal lines]]
- Lets go over some useful terminology:
	- Angles that lie in-between the 2 [[parallel]] lines are called interior [[Angles]]
	- Angles that lie on the outside are called exterior angles.
- Alternate interior angles: -> These angles are the interior angles that are related through correspondence and being [[Vertical Angle]] they are [[congruent]]
	- $\angle 3$ and $\angle 6$, $\angle 4$ and $\angle 5$ 
- Alternate Exterior angles -> These angles are the exterior angles that are related through correspondence and being [[Vertical Angle\|Vertical angles]], they are [[congruent]]
	- $\angle 1$ and $\angle 8$, $\angle 2$ and $\angle 7$
- Same side interior angles -> These angles are NOT [[congruent]], what they are is literally in the name.
	- $\angle 3$ and $\angle 5$, $\angle 4$ and $\angle 6$
	- Instead of being congruent these [[Angles]] are actually [[Straight angles\|supplementary angles]]. This is because $1$ and $2$ are supplementary ([[linear pair theorem]]) so through [[Vertical Angle\|Vertical Angle theorem]] $1=4$ and $2=6$ because they are corresponding
	- This shows that $4$ and $6$ are supplementary.
