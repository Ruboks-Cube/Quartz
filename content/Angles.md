## What are angles
source: https://artofproblemsolving.com/videos/prealgebra/chapter10/197 -- introduction to angles AOPS

- When two [[Line Segment]]s come together at a single point, an angle is formed. Another way to put this is that there are angles at [[vertex]]es.
- We measure angles in [[degrees]]
	- The max measurement is 360, and the smallest is 0
	- 360 degree angles look like a line, but one of the [[Line Segment]]s moved in a [[Circle]] motion on the point it was attached to, to stop at the other line
	- When we open an angle the degree gets higher, closing it makes it smaller
Soutce: Edmunten
- When 2 [[line\|lines]] or [[Line Segment\|Line Segments]] - [[Intersect]] at a [[point]], a quantity that describes the intersection is the amount that will turn (rotate) one [[line]] onto the other about the [[point]] they crossed. This quantity is called the [[Angles\|Angle]] 
	- The formal [[Definition]] of an [[Angles\|Angle]] is as two [[Ray\|rays]] that extend from a common [[point]]. 
	- The common point is called a [[vertex]] of the angle. 
	- If 2 [[Line Segment\|Line Segments]] share a common endpoint and their other endpoints lie in opposite directions from each other, then the measure of the angle between the line segments is $180^{\circ}$. This is known as a [[Straight angles\|supplementary angle]]
		- If the uncommon endpoints lie in the same direction then the angle measure is $0^{\circ}$, you move the line $0$ degrees to put it on the "other" line
- The angle between 2 line segments only depends on the orientation of the line segments. The length of the line segments do not matter.
	- If you move the non-common endpoints of the line segments closer or farther straight towards or straight away then the angle will not change.
- ! A practice problem:
	- Question: ![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240627065758.png|500]]
	- Answer:
		- The answer is that $\angle DAB$ will be equal to $\angle CAB$ because the lengths of the line segments do not matter. [[point\|Point]] $D$ is just [[point]] $C$ moved closer to $A$ in a straight-towards fashion so it does not change the angle.
