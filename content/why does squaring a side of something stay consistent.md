#math/arithmetic/exponent/radical 

Why is it that
$$x=y$$
is equivalent to 
$$x^2=y^2$$
Or a better case would be
$$x<y=x^2<y^2$$
We are multiplying the sides by different numbers, when we multiply x by x, and y by y, they are increasing by different values, multiplying by themself.


You are multiplying x by itself, and y by itself, since y is greater than x, it will grow more than x, so the gap between them will increase, but the relationship that x is less than y, is kept the same.
