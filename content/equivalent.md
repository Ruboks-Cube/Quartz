Equivalent means the same. 

### Fractions

[[Fraction]]s can sometimes be equivalent, just like how [[Ratio]]s can be [[Proportion]]al. 

Equivalent fractions happen when both the [[Numerator]] and the [[Denominator]] can be [[Division\|Divided]]/ [[Multiplication\|Multiplied]] by the same amount to get a new fraction. That new fraction is the same as the older fraction. Here is why:

![[Files/Images/equivalent 2024-01-18 18.57.24.excalidraw]]
Here you can see that multiplying both the numerator and the denominator by 2 so we get 1/2 to 2/4 makes the number of pieces to change, from 2, to 4, but also the amount of pieces taken doubles, making it so 2 pieces are taken and it's equal to 1/2.

Another way to think about it is that you're essentially multiplying the fraction by 1 since you are multiplying the numerator and denominator by the same number, you are essentially doing 1 fraction times a whole fraction, and a whole fraction is 1, and whenever you multiply anything by 1, it stays the SAME.
