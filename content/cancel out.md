#math #math/algebra/inequality/equations #math/arithmetic/divisionmultiplication/fraction , [[Expression]], [[Fraction]]


To cancel out in math means in [[Multiplication]] or [[Division]] to be equal to one, or in [[addition]] or [[subtraction]] to be equal to 0.

For example, $6-6$, the 6 and the $-6$ both cancel out to equal 0.

An example in [[Division]] would be like $\frac{6}{6}*2$, where the $\frac{6}{6}$ cancels out to become $1$, so we are left with $1*2$ which is the same as just having $2$. 


> [!warning] Addition and multiplication are different when cancelling out things!
> Say we have the [[Fraction]] $\frac{{4+5x}}{4}$ Some might say that we can simplify it to $5x$ since the 4's cancel out, but since $\frac{{4+5x}}{4}$ does not equal $\frac{4}{4}* \frac{{{5x}{}}}{4}$ we can't do that.  
>
