#math/geometry/2d/shape/Triangle/trigonometry #math/graphing 

- Frame shifts are when you add or subtract something to $x$ 
	- [[Graphing Trigonometric Functions]]
- For example: $y=\sin(\theta-\pi)$
	- This causes the whole graph to shift to the right by $\pi$ 
	- Why right?? Literally every other [[function]] in math does this
	- It’s because the $x$ value is happening *later* so it moves right on the graph
- In cases where there is a frame shift, before [[identifying the period]] you must do something
	- Say you have $y=3\sin\left( 4\theta-\frac{\pi}{2} \right)$
		- You need to  factor out the $4$ first
		- $y=3\sin\left( 4\left( \theta-\frac{\pi}{8} \right) \right)$
		- why? 
		- [[Factoring]] makes it so the [[Coefficient]] of $\theta$ is $1$, so we can correctly find out how much we are adding/subtracting to “$x$” beforehand
