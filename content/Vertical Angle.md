## What is a vertical angle?
- Vertical angle theorem: If 2 angles are made by [[Intersect\|Intersecting lines]] those angles on the opposite side are congruent.
	- Why?
	- Lets say we have 2 lines that intersect and 4 angles are created. $\angle a, \angle b, \angle c, \angle d$
	- Lets say that $\angle a$ and $\angle b$ are vertical angles, and $\angle c$ and $\angle d$ are the other pair of vertical angles
	- Through [[linear pair theorem]] we know that $\angle b$ is [[Straight angles\|supplementary]] to $\angle c$ so $b+c=180$
	- $a$ is [[adjacent]] to $c$ as well and that means that $a+c=180$ as well meaning that through [[Transitive Property]] $a+c=b+c$. 
	- From [[Subtraction property of equality]] we know that $a=b$.

If you have 2 line segments that intersect at a certain point, angles opposite to each other will be equal
