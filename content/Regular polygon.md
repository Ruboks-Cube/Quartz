#math/geometry/2d/shape #math/geometry 

- A regular [[Polygon]] is a polygon who's [[Line Segment\|side lengths]] and [[Angles]] are all the same. Think [[Perfect Squares\|Square]], [[Triangle]], [[Circle]].

### Rotation aligning
- Something interesting about these polygons is that if you have a center or [[Rotation]] on their center they map back on themself more than once.
- The second you alter a regular polygon's side length, or angle it will not map back on itself perfectly more than once.
	- When a regular polygon is rotated the number of times it will map back on itself in 1 full rotation is equal to the number of [[Line Segment\|sides]] it has. ![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240627121303.png]]
