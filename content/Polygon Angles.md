## Angles in a quadrilateral 
Why is a [[quadrilateral]]s angles always equal to 360 degrees?
We know that a [[Triangle]]s angles are always equal to [[180 degree angle\|180 degrees]], and 360 is 180 times 2.
The reason a quadrilaterals angles are equal to 360 degrees is because you can break any quadrilateral down into 2 triangles.
![[Files/Images/Polygon Angles 2024-02-11 16.54.53.excalidraw]]
By connecting 2 opposite [[vertex\|vertices]], we make 2 triangles.

## N sides

![[Files/Images/Polygon Angles 2024-02-11 17.17.47.excalidraw]]
We know that the angles of a polygon
are just the amount of triangles times 180.
So how do we find out how many triangles
there are in a polygon?

In any polygon, we can choose a vertex to make a point for every triangle we can make. Here you can see that is vertex A. 
- Every polygon has n sides, and we can break down that polygon into n-2 triangles
- Where does that n-2 come from?
	- In each triangle, there is 1 side opposite of vertex a
	- In any side that A is part of, except for the ones adjacent to A, we can draw a triangle from
	- We cannot draw a triangle from the sides right next to A, but we can with any other side
	- So n-2 sides are left where we can draw a triangle from.
