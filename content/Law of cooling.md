#science/physics #math #math/function 

- [[Isaac Newton\|Newton’s]] law of cooling is $y=(T_{0}-A)e^{-kt}+A$
- Starting temperature minus ambient temperature times e to the power of negative rate times time + ambient temperature. 


> [!danger] Problem
> A cup of fast-food coffee is $180^{\circ}F$ when freshly poured. After 2 minutes in a room at $70^{\circ}F$, the coffee has cooled to $165^{\circ}F$. Find the time that it will take for the coffee to cool to $120^{\circ}F$.

- $(180-70)e^{-2k}+70=165$
- $110e^{-2k}=95$
