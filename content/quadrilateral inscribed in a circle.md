- In [[quadrilateral]] $QRST$ $\angle Q=68^{\circ}$, $\angle R=(3x+40^{\circ})$,  $\angle T=(5x-52)$
	- What is are the measures of $\angle R$, ${} \angle S$, and $\angle R$?
	- We know that $\angle Q+\angle S=180^{\circ}$, so we have $68^{\circ}+\angle S=180^{\circ}$
		- Solving for $\angle S$ gives-> ${} 112^{\circ} {}$
	- We know that $\angle R+\angle T=180^{\circ}$ -> $(3x+40)+(5x-52)=180^{\circ}$ 
		- Solve the [[Linear Equation]]
		- $8x-12=180^{\circ}$
		- $8x=192$
		- $x=24$ 
	- Plug in $x$ for $\angle R$'s [[Expression]]
		- $\angle R=3(24)+40=112$
	- $\angle R+\angle T=180^{\circ}$
		- $112^{\circ}+\angle T=180^{\circ}$ 
		- $\angle T=68^{\circ}$
	- $\angle R,\angle S,\angle T= 112^{\circ},112^{\circ}, 68^{\circ}$
