# What does this mean?
Sometimes linear equations have either no solutions, or an *infinite* amount of solutions.
 
### Example of no solutions
![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240416193509.png]]
- (a) $2z+3-4=3z-5-z$, combine like terms to get $2x-1=2x-5$, subtract 2x from both sides and they cancel out, giving us $-1=-5$. This means that there are *no* solutions in this [[Linear Equation]] since the left side is 4 more than the right side always, if you imagine a scale, this is not equal.

### Example of infinite solution
![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240416193517.png]]
- (b) combine like terms to get $4r+5=4r+5$.  Both sides are the exact same, meaning that *any* value of $r$ would be a solution to this equation, showing an infinite amount of solutions.
