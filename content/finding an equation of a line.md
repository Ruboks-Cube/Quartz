## Key points
- We can rearrange the [[slope]] equation to get [[point-slope form]] or [[standard form for 2 variable linear equations]].
- We can find the equation of a [[line]] with 2 [[ordered pair\|ordered pairs]] or 1 [[ordered pair]] and the lines [[slope]]. In both cases, we can find the slope by [[Calculating Slope]] (unless we already have it) and [[setting something equal to something else\|set it equal to]] $\frac{{y-y_{1}}}{x-x_{1}}$ where $x,y$ are just [[variable math\|variables]] (we are trying to find a [[2 variable linear equations\|2 variable equation here]]) and $x_{1},y_{1}$ is an [[ordered pair]] given to us
	- Check your answer by plugging in an [[ordered pair]]

## 8.14
![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240609130246.png]]
- (a) the points $(-1,-7)$, $(0,-2)$, and $(1,3)$ are on the line. (These are not all the possible answers)
- (b) the slope is $5$, we find this by [[Calculating Slope]]. This makes sense because the line is steep from left to right. 
- (c) the slope would be $\frac{{y-3}}{x-1}$ 
- (d) The [[slope]] for this graph is $5$, so $\frac{{y-3}}{x-1}=5$
- (e) We can rearrange the equation by first multiplying both sides by $x-1$. This gives $y-3=5(x-1)$. Expanding and rearranging gives $5x-y=2$. 
	- We can also write this equation as $5x=y+2$ or as $y=5x-2$, or as any other equation that can be rearranged back to $5x=y+2$. One way we deal with many different ways to write an equation is to define a small handful of useful forms of it. For example, the definition of slope is a commonly used equation, $\frac{{y-y_{1}}}{x-x_{1}}=m$. 
	- The definition of [[slope]] can fail us when $(x,y)$ is the same point as $(x_{1},y_{1})$ since the left side would just be undefined, so we fix this by multiplying both sides of the [[equations\|Equation]] by $x-x_{1}$ to give $y-y_{1}=m(x-x_{1})$.  This is called [[point-slope form]] of the equation.
	- In (e) of this problem we rearranged the [[point-slope form]] into [[standard form for 2 variable linear equations]], $Ax+By=C$, notice how this looks similar to [[Inverse Proportion]].

## 8.15
![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240610061824.png]]
- ~ Solution
	- We know how to find the equation of a line given its slope and a point on the line, so we find the [[slope]]!
	- The equation for slope gives us $m=\frac{{y_{1}-y_{2}}}{x_{1}-y_{2}}$, the slope for this would be $\frac{{-3-4}}{5-0}=-\frac{7}{5}$ 
		- Like before, if $(x,y)$ is on the line and $(x,y)$ is not $(0,4)$ we have $\frac{{y-4}}{x-0}=-\frac{7}{5}$ 
	- Simplifying this equation gives $7x+5y=20$, which is our equation
