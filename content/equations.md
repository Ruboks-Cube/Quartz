# What is an equation?
Imagine a scale in which both objects placed on either side make the scale balanced. To weigh an object, we place the unknown weight on one side while we keep adding weights on the other side until the scale is balanced.

Take this example:
![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240406100334.png]]
Here the scale is balanced, since $1+5+1=7$, we know the other side's weight must be equal to 7.

We just wrote an equation, which is when 2 mathematical [[Expression\|Expressions]] are equal.

Another, more better way to explain it is to actually go through the steps of multiplying 301 and 43 to both sides.

$\frac{73}{43}=\frac{511}{301}$, multiply the denominators by both sides we get $\frac{73*301*43}{43}=\frac{511*43*301}{301}$
We will cancel out the 43's on the left side and the 301's on the right side to get
$73*301=511*43$. This known as [[cross multiplying]]
