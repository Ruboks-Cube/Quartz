## Rotation aligning for regular polygons
### Rotation aligning
- Something interesting about these polygons is that if you have a center or [[Rotation]] on their center they map back on themself more than once.
- The second you alter a regular polygon's side length, or angle it will not map back on itself perfectly more than once.
	- When a regular polygon is rotated the number of times it will map back on itself in 1 full rotation is equal to the number of [[Line Segment\|sides]] it has. ![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240627121303.png]]

## Irregular polygons rotation aligning?
- Polygons that are irregular have fewer [[Rotation\|Rotations]] that make it so they map back on themselves than [[Regular polygon\|Regular Polygons]]. 
	- For example, a [[Perfect Squares\|Square]] maps back on itself 4 times, turned counterclockwise or clockwise through the [[Right Angle\|Right angles]] at the center. 
- A [[Rectangle]] does not follow this rule however, it doesn't have the full symmetry a square does. A rectangle only maps back on itself at $180^{\circ}$ and $360^{\circ}$ ![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240627122247.png]]
- Similar to rectangles, [[parallelogram\|Parallelograms]] map back on themself twice as well.
- Something like a [[Trapezoid]] only has 1 pair of [[parallel]] sides, the non parallel sides may or may not be equal in length. The lack of symmetry of a trapezoid does not allow the shape to map onto itself more than once through the full rotation.
