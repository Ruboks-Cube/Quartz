## Generating TWO quadratic equations
- Use of [[Quadratic Regression on Ti-84+\|QuadReg]]
	- $y=0.899x^2-0.819x+0.652$
- Use of $(0,0) \text{ and }(6,29)$ 
	- $y=\frac{29}{36}x^2$ 
	- [[Standard Form quadratic]] and [[Vertex Form]] look very similar for this equation
### Analysis
- *a value*
	- $\frac{29}{36}\approx 0.806$ → from [[Vertex Form]]
	- $0.899$ → from [[Quadratic Regression on Ti-84+\|QuadReg]]
- *b value*
	- $0$ → from [[Vertex Form]]
	- $-0.819$ → from QuadReg
- *c value*
	- $0$ → from Vertex Form
	- $0.652$ → from QuadReg

- The QuadReg model will be more accurate for predictions past the point (0,0) because it takes account ALL of the points in the data set while $y=\frac{29}{36}x^2$ is only taking into account $(0,0)$ and $(6,29)$
- $y=0.899x^2-0.819x+0.652$ predicts that at a diameter of 20 cheerios 343.794
- $100=0.899x^2-0.819x+0.652$

	- With $x$ representing the [[diameter]] of the circle and $y$ representing the [[Area]].
	- The area of a circle is $\pi r^2$, so we have $\pi \left( \frac{x}{2} \right)^2$ since $x$ is the diameter instead of the [[radius]]
	- Squaring gives us $\pi  \frac{x^2}{4}$
- Using $3.14=\pi$ to graph the theoretical model, it’s similar to the previous 2 models.
	- This model *would* be better in theory since it’s using the actual relationship between the [[diameter]] and [[Area]] but it is difficult to tell if it would be better to predict the amount of cheerios the area would be based on the diameter any better than the other two.
