#math/algebra/inequality/equations/Ratios_Proportions_Rates , [[Proportion]]

- [[Direct Proportion]] is when the quotient, or [[Ratio]] of 2 variables is always constant, or the same. Inverse proportion means that the product is constant. We sometimes say that such quantities  "In inverse proportion"

- To say that something is inversely proportional is to say that $x$ and $y$ are being multiplied
> [!question] Why?
> *Inverse* means opposite, contrary.
> 
> We recall that in inverse proportion $xy=k$
> 
> as $x$ increases, $y$ must decrease in order to account for the increase of $x$. 
> The 2 [[variable math\|variables]] are *opposing* each other.

# Problems
## Key Ideas
- If $x$ and $y$ are inversely proportional then the product $xy$ is constant. We can write this as $xy=k$. Sometimes, $k$ is referred to as the constant of proportionality.
- As $x$ goes up, $y$ comes down, and vice versa. When we multiply $x$ by something, we multiply $y$ by the [[Reciprocal]] of what we multiplied $x$ by.
## 7.4
- We start with a few examples, as we did in [[Direct Proportion]]
![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240518102227.png]]
- (a) Solution 1 for problem a *find the constant product*
	- $p$ and $q$ are inversely proportional, meaning that $pq=z$. We know that when $p=7$, $q=24$. Since $p$ and $q$ are inversely proportional, we get $7*24=168$, so $pq=168$. If $q=12$, we have $12p=168$.
		- Solving for $p$ gives $p=14$
	- Solution 2 for problem a, *One goes up, the other goes down*
		- Since the product of $pq$ must remain constant when we halve $q$ from $24$ to $12$, we double $p$ to keep the product constant. Therefore, when $q=\frac{24}{2}$, $p=7*2$, so $p=14$
		- This solution is very similar to the [[Ratios come in groups\|Groupings]] solutions in [[Direct Proportion]]. The difference is that when one variable gets multiplied by a factor, the other one gets multiplied by the [[Reciprocal]] of the factor for the other [[variable math\|variable]]
- (b) $w^2$ and $z^3$ are inversely proportional, so the product of $w^2z^3$ remains constant.
	- When we have $w^2z^3$ when $w=3$ and $z=12$, we have $3^212^3$. When we have $w^2z^3$ for $w=6$, we have $6^2z^3$. The product remains constant, so we have $3^212^3=6^2z^3$. 
	- Solving for $z$ gives $6\sqrt [3]{2}$. 
## 7.5
- Inverse proportionality doesn't pop up in real life as much as [[Direct Proportion]] but it has it's moments
![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240518104747.png]]
- Solution: First, we determine how the number of workers and hours needed are related. $12$ people clear a field in $18$ hours, so the $12$ people clear $\frac{1}{18}$ of the field in an hour. Of the $12$ people, $1$ person can clear $\frac{1}{216}$ of the field in an hour. 
	- We know that $x$ people can clear $\frac{1}{216}x=\frac{x}{216}$ of the field in an hour. 
	- In $y$ hours, the $x$ people clear $\frac{x}{216}y$ every hour. The [[equations\|Equation]] for when the field will be cleared would be $\frac{x}{216}y=1$. Multiplying both sides by $216$ gives $xy=216$. The number of people times the number of hours is 216.
	- Plug in $9$ for the number of people to get $9y=216$. $\frac{216}{9}=24$ hours.
- We could have also solved this problem by noticing that we go from $12$ people, to $9$ people. We're multiplying $12$ by $\frac{3}{4}$ so we multiply $18$ be $\frac{4}{3}$ (the [[Reciprocal]]) to get $24$. 
## 7.6
![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240518110215.png]]
- We have ${} xy=a {}$ and $yz=b$. We want to see how $x$ and $z$ are related. 
- We can try solving for $y$ in the first equation to get $y=\frac{a}{x}$. Plugging this in for $y$ in the second equation gives $\frac{a}{x}z=b$. Multiplying both sides by $x$ gives $az=bx$. Dividing both sides by $a$ gives $z=\frac{bx}{a}$, which is equal to $z=bx* \frac{1}{a}$. Dividing both sides by $x$ gives $\frac{z}{x}=\frac{b}{a}$, showing that the quotient of $z$ and $x$ is equal to $\frac{b}{a}$, and therefore, $x$ and $z$ are [[Direct Proportion\|Directly proportional]]
