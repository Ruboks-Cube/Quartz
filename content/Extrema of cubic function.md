#math/algebra/inequality/equations/polynomial #math/algebra/inequality/equations/polynomial/cubic


- In a [[Cubic]] [[math functions\|function]] a formula to find if there are extrema or not is $b^2-3ac$
	- If $b^2-3ac > 0$ it has extrema
	- $=0$ it plateaus
	- $<0$ it has no extrema


- why is this?
	- [[how each term of a polynomial affects the graph]]
- We know the $b$ term wants that [[Parabola\|parabolic]] shape
	- the $a$ term wants a [[Cubic]] shape
	- the $c$ term wants a [[line]] shape
- Both the $a$ and $c$ term are working against the $b$ term to get that [[Parabola]]
- This means that it must be a difference of $ac$ versus $b$, so we have $b-ac$ 
	- However this is not the true formula
- If each of the coefficients had an equal “weight” then this would be correct, but it’s not, because the $a$ term is the strongest and the $c$ term is the weakest
