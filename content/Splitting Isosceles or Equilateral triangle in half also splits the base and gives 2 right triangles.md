#math/geometry/2d/shape/Triangle , [[Right Triangle]]

When we split an [[isosceles triangle]] or a [[Equilateral Triangle]] into 2 halves the base is also split in half and the 2 sides are equal.

![[Files/Images/Splitting Isosceles or Equilateral triangle in half also splits the base 2024-02-26 18.18.02.excalidraw]]
We split $\overline{b}$ into two sides with $\overline{h}$, $x$ and $y$.
We know that $x^2+h^2=a^2$ and $y^2+h^2=a^2$ because $a$ and $h$ are the same.


- so we know that $\triangle axh$ and $\triangle ayh$ are both [[congruent]] from [[SSS side side side]].
- We can also intuitively tell that the angle between line segment $xh$ and $yh$ are $90^{\circ}$. You prove it though
