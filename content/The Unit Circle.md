#math/geometry/2d/shape/Triangle/trigonometry #math/geometry/2d/shape/circle 


- The Unit Circle is a [[Circle]] with the center at [[origin]] and the [[radius]] is $1$
	- This helps with [[Trigonometry]]
	- On the Unit Circle:
		- $\sin \theta=y$ → $\csc \theta=\frac{1}{y}$ 
		- $\cos \theta=x$ → ${} \sec \theta=\frac{1}{x} {}$
		- $\tan \theta=\frac{y}{x}$ → $\cot \theta=\frac{x}{y}$ 
- Focus on the first quadrant
	![[Files/The Unit Circle 2025-05-12 19.34.19.excalidraw]]
- We use [[Excalidraw/Special Right Triangles\|Special Right Triangles]] and the fact that the [[radius]] (and therefore the [[Hypotenuse]] is 1) to get the points
	- The $x$ coordinate will always be the [[adjacent]] side 
	- The $y$ coordinate is the opposite side
- The rest of the quadrants look JUST like this but reflected across the [[axis]] 
	- The angle measures will be different  but you go by 30, 45, 60
	- Remember that you’re REFLECTING  and go in the CLOCKWISE motion
