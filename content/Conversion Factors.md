# Book
- Sometimes we are given information in one unit, like feet, but we actually need it to be in other units, like inches
	- conversion factors are our easy way to convert to one unit to another
- **A conversion factor is a ratio of quantities that is equal to one**
	- for example: $\frac{1ft}{12in}=1$, this is a conversion factor
		- We have a ratio of feet to inches that is equal to 1, since 1 ft and 12 in are the same thing, dividing one by the other gives 1
		- Multiplying anything by 1 leaves that quantity unchanged, so we can multiply anything by our conversion factor and leave it unchanged
## Problems
### The basics
#### Key ideas
- We start with a conversion factor like $\frac{1ft}{12in}$
	- If we are trying to convert one unit to another unit and have our conversion factor, we just strategically multiply by 1, putting the unit we want in the [[Numerator]] and the unit we don't want in the [[Denominator]] 
	- the unit we don't want [[cancel out]] and we are left with the unit we want
	- If we have a conversion factor that does not cancel out properly, just take the [[Reciprocal]] and then multiply it, simple as that

## 6.9
- Key ideas
	- in this problem we have to convert it to a different [[Exponents\|Power]], we do this by multiplying by the conversion factor multiple times
![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240506204947.png]]
- Solution:
	- We wish to convert $28m^2$ into some number of $f^2$
		- We have $\frac{3.28ft}{1m}=1$ 
	- We have $28m^2$, and we have to multiply it to get $ft^2$
		- We do this by multiplying $28m^2$ by $\frac{3.28ft}{1m}$ twice, so that the $m$ actually cancels out
	- This leaves us with:
		- $28m^2* \frac{3.28ft}{1m}* \frac{3.28ft}{1m}$
		- This gives $301ft^2$

## 6.11
- Key Ideas
	- Any time we have 2 equivalent quantities, we can make a conversion factor out of them, in [[Conversion Factors#6.11\|6.11]] we use gallons to $ft^2$ as a conversion factor since we have a ratio of gallons to feet$^2$ covered since these two are [[equivalent]], $300ft^2=1$ gallon and vice versa 
![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240507202350.png]]
- Solution
	- In this problem, we have to find a ratio of $370m^2$: $cans$ of paint.
	- We don't have square meters to cans of paint conversion factor, but we have $\frac{1g}{300ft^2}=1$ 
		- We can first convert $370m^2$ into feet, so our problem deals with only 1 unit of space (we could also convert $300ft^2$ into meters)
		- One gallon of paint convers $300^2$ feet so we can find a ratio between $370m^2$ and gallons (but we first convert $370m^2$ to $ft^2$)
		- We then turn gallons to liters and then finally liters to cans
	- Square meters, to square feet, to gallons, to liters to cans
		- First we convert square meters to square feet as we did in the problem [[Conversion Factors#6.9\|6.9]]
			- We are given $\frac{3.28ft}{1m}=1$ and we have $370m^2$
			- Multiply $370m^2$ by $\frac{3.28ft}{1m}$ twice and we get $3980ft^2$
		- Next, we find the [[Ratio]] of $3980ft^2$ to gallons, 
			- We do this by using our conversion factor of $\frac{1g}{300ft^2}=1$ 
			- We find how many gallons of paint we need from having $\frac{3980ft^2*1g}{300ft^2}$, the $ft^2$ both [[cancel out]] leaving us with $13.3g$
			- So we know that $13.3g=3980ft^2$
		- convert gallons to liters by getting out conversion factor $\frac{1L}{0.264g}=1$
			- We multiply $13.3g$ by $\frac{1L}{0.264g}$ to get $50.4L$
		- Now we know that $50.4L=3980ft^2$
		- Finally, Convert Liters to cans, $\frac{1c}{4L}=1$ since the problem says each can holds 4 liters of paint
			- We multiply $50.4L$ by $\frac{1c}{4L}$ to get $12.6$ cans
	- We round up $12.6$ cans to $13$ since getting $.6$ of a can does NOT make sense here

#### Simple one, feet to inches
There are 12 inches in a foot. Other guy is 5 and a half feet tall, how tall is he in inches?

There is 5 feet which is 60 inches because 5 [[Multiplication\|multiplied]] by is 60, then tack on another half feet and we get 66 inches.

Here we simply use [[Proportion]], since there is 12 inches in a foot, that is a ratio. For every 12 inches, there is 1 foot. Now we need to know how many inches are in 5 and a half feet, we multiplied 1 foot by 5 and a half, so our [[Scale Factor]] is 5 1/2, so we multiply 12 by that and get 66.

#### Kilograms to pounds 
One kilogram is approximately 2.2 pounds, other guy is approximately 150 pounds, how many kilograms is that?

It may not be obvious where we multiply or divide here, this is where conversion factors come into play.

First we set 1kg equal to 2.2lb (lb is pounds), this sets up a [[Proportion]], 1kg:2.2lb, but it's just written differently.

$$1\operatorname{kg}=2.2pounds$$
[[Division\|Dividing]] both sides by 2.2 pounds we get: 
$$\frac{1\operatorname{kg}}{2.2pounds}=1$$
This is the [[Ratio]], 1kg:2.2lb. This shows that they are equal, you could also divide by 1kg instead.

$$\frac{1kilo}{2.2pound}=1=\frac{2.2pound}{1kilo}$$
We now have our conversion factors, kilo and pounds are [[variable math\|Variables]] with different values, all we know is that 1 kilo is 2.2 pounds.
Now, we want 150 pounds and convert it into kilograms, here is how.

We take the conversion factor which will cancel out the one we already have by strategically multiplying by 1. 

Since we have 150 pounds, we multiply it by 1kilo/2.2pounds so the pounds cancel out and we're left with kilograms. 

$$150pounds\cdot\frac{1kilo}{2.2pounds}=\frac{150}{2.2}kilo$$
The pounds cancel out, and 150/2.2 = 68

You can also do:

150/2.2 to find the [[Scale Factor]] = around 68
1 kg times 68 = 68.

### Problems in source

1 U.S dollar equals 80 japanese yen. Will is going to Japan, he is told he will need 60,000 yen while he is there. How many U.S dollars does he need to convert to Yen?

Of course, we could use [[Proportion]] with this problem.

1dollar:80 yen 
xdollar:60,000
60,000/80 = 750

1dollar times 750 = 750

he will need to bring 750 U.S dollars.

However, we can use conversion factors as well.

1 U.S dollar = 80 Japanese Yen

Needs 60,000 Yen

$$\frac{1dollar\cdot60,000yen}{80yen}$$
Simplifying the above expression equals:
$$750dollars$$
So we basically did the whole thing in 2 steps... Nice
