#math/geometry/2d/shape 


- Isosceles [[Triangle\|Triangles]] are triangles that have 2 of the same side lengths, but one of the side lengths are different.
	- [[Splitting Isosceles or Equilateral triangle in half also splits the base and gives 2 right triangles]]

- Isosceles Triangles also have properties with [[isosceles right triangle]].
- If the 2 sides of an isosceles triangle are congruent, how are the [[Angles]] related?
	- [[bisector]]
- The proof:
	- We let $\triangle ABC$ be an isosceles [[Triangle]] where $AB=BC$ as shown:                          ![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240706094402.png]]
		- We create point $D$ on $\overline {AC}$ so $\overline {BD}$ bisects $\angle ABC$ -> Constructing angle bisector
		- $\angle ABD=\angle DBC$ -> [[Definition]] of [[Angles\|Angle]] bisector
		- $BD=BD$ -> [[Reflexive property of equality]]
		- $\triangle ABD \cong \triangle CBD$ -> [[SAS side angle side]]
		- $\angle BAC=\angle BCA$ -> [[corresponding angles]] of [[congruent]] triangles have equal measures
