## Key Ideas and What is slope? 
- ! Slope is a [[Ratio]] of the difference of the $y$ values to the difference of the $x$ values. [[Calculating Slope]]
- ? What does slope mean? It means that for every time $x$ changes by any value $a$ then $y$ will change by $b$ for $\frac{{y_{1}-y_{2}}}{x_{1}-x_{2}}=\frac{a}{b}$. All it's saying is that for $b$ change in $x$ there is $a$ change in $y$. 
- Why does this matter? First it tells us the "direction" of the line. Remember [[Graphing Linear Equations\|Hopsalot]]? He hops 1 right, 2 up right so our slope is $\frac{2}{1}$? And so when $x$ is $1$ $y$ is $2$. When $x$ is $2$ $y$ is $4$. $x=8$ $y=16$. Here we see that the [[Ratio]] of $y$ to $x$ is the same as the difference of $y$ to the difference of $x$. 
- To get why negative slopes go down, and positive slopes go up, consider the points. For positive slope, as $x$ gets bigger, $y$ also gets bigger, so the numerator and denominator of $\frac{y_{2}-y_{1}}{x_{2}-x_{1}}$ would have to be the same sign. For negative slopes, the sign of the numerator and the denominator is different. This means that as $x$ gets bigger $y$ gets smaller.
- When slope is positive, the line goes up. When slope is negative, the line goes down. When slope is 0, it's a horizontal line. When slope is undefined, the line is vertical.
- ! Slope can be thought of as a measure of steepness. A line with a large positive slope is very "steep" meaning that it's close to a vertical line. A line with a small vertical slope like $0$ or $\frac{1}{10}$ is not steep, it's like a horizontal line. We can do this for negative slopes, the less the [[Absolute Value]] of the slope the less steep, the more the [[Absolute Value]] of the slope the more steep.
