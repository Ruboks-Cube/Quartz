#math/geometry 

- A perpendicular bisector is a [[line]], [[Ray]], or [[Line Segment\|segment]] that intersects another [[Line Segment]] at the [[Finding the midpoint between 2 points\|Midpoint]] between the 2 endpoints of the line segment at a [[Right Angle\|90 degree angle]] so the 2 lines are [[Perpendicular]]
	- EG: ![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240703214154.png|Notice here that CE and CD are both Rays|300]]
	- How does a perpendicular bisector relate to the [[endpoints]] of the [[Line Segment]]?
- Perpendicular bisector theorem:
	- If a point lies on the perpendicular bisector of a [[Line Segment]] then it's equidistant from both [[endpoints]] on the segment.
	- Given $\overleftrightarrow{CD}$ is a perpendicular bisector of $\overline{AB}$ prove that any point on $CD$ is equidistant from the endpoints of $\overline {AB}$ 
		- $CD$ is perpendicular bisector of $AB$ -> given
		- We let $P$ be any point on $CD$ -> Defining an arbitrary point
		- We let $E$ represent the [[Finding the midpoint between 2 points\|midpoint]] of $AB$
		- $\angle PEA=90^{\circ}$ and $\angle PEB=90^{\circ}$ -> Definition of perpendicular bisector
		- $\angle {PEA}=\angle PEB$ -> [[Transitive Property]]
		- $AE=BE$ -> Definition of perpendicular bisector using point. 
		- $PE=PE$ -> [[Reflexive property of equality]]
		- $\triangle {PEA}=\triangle PEB$ [[SAS side angle side]] criterion for [[congruent]] [[Triangle\|Triangles]] 
		- $\overline {PA} = \overline {PB}$ -> [[corresponding parts]] of [[congruent]] triangles are [[congruent]]
		- $PA=PB$ -> definition of [[congruent]]
