# Numbers

## 2 and 5
### example problem
![[Files/Images/2, 4, 5, and 25 for divisibily 2023-12-22 20.21.41.excalidraw]]
We are using the [[Multiples added to Multiples]] rule to find that N must be a multiple of 2, like 2,4,6,8,0 so 1743N is divisible by 2. 

Since 1743 times 10 is a multiple of 2 as seen in the image, we need N to be a multiple of 2 to make 1743N a multiple of 2. 


This is also a rule for 5! Since 10 is a multiple of 5, what we are doing is adding a multiple of 5 to N, so N must be a multiple of 5, (0 or 5) so 1743N is divisible by 5.
# 4 and 25
1743N, for what values of N is 1743N divisible by 4.
![[Files/Images/2, 4, 5, and 25 for divisibily 2023-12-22 20.35.44.excalidraw]]
What we did here was instead look at multiples of 100, putting the 3 and N together. Since 100 is a [[Multiple]] of 4, 17400 is a multiple of 4, now all we had to worry about was 3N being a multiple of 4, and we know that 32 and 36 is a multiple of 4, so 2 and 6 are the possible values for N. We used the [[Multiples added to Multiples]] rule.

ALSO since 25 is a multiple of 100, we need to look at the last 2 digits to check is 25 is divisible, since we are sure there is a multiple of 100, and if the last 2 digits = a multiple of 25, (25, 50, 75, 00), then yeah! It's divisible by 25 because of the [[Multiples added to Multiples]]
