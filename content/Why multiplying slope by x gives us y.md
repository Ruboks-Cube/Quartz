#math/graphing [[Cartesian coordinate system]], [[slope]]

## Long Ahh Explanation
- It's change in $y$ over change in $x$ so yeah it's a [[Ratio]]. Lets say the [[slope]] is $\frac{7}{2}$, when $x$ changes by $2$, the $y$ changes by $7$. What if $x$ changes by $3$? Then what happens? This is where multiplying the slope comes in, because [[Ratio\|ratios]] and [[Fraction\|Fractions stay equivalent]]. We first pretend the [[Ratio]] is when $x=1$, that would mean that we divide $\frac{7}{2}$ to make the ratio equivalent, the answer to this is just $3.5$. This means that our ratio is $3.5:1$, so if $x=3$ we multiply $3.5$ by $3$ as well. Hence, multiplying the by $x$ gives us $y$!
- ? So what does this mean for all our other [[line\|Lines]]?
	- Lets say the slope is $\frac{z}{w}$, our ratio between change in $y$ to change in $x$ is $z:w=\frac{z}{w}$ 
	- ! **THIS IS THE IMPORTANT PART PLS REMEMBER:** If we want $w$ to equal $1$, we have $\frac{\frac{z}{w}}{1}$ to keep the [[Ratio]] - [[equivalent]]. This again simplifies to just $\frac{z}{w}$ since that's all that matters, so this is our scale, our [[unit rate]] which we can multiply by $x$ our ([[Scale Factor]]). 
- ! When we change $x$ by 1 and the slope is $a:b$ all we're doing is making $b=1$ so then the left side of the ratio is change in y over change in x.
