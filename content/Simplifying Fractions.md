# What is simplifying fractions
We know that some [[Fraction\|Fractions]] are [[equivalent]], but how do we get to the simpler fractions?

Lets start with a fraction like $\frac{yx}{y}$. Since we see the [[Denominator]] in the [[Numerator]] we can [[cancel out]].

What about something like this? $\frac{2yx}{y}$. Here we have again, the denominator in the numerator, so we cancel out the $y$ to get $2x$.

Okay, but what if we have $\frac{\frac{1}{2}{yx}}{y}$?

Again, in this situation, the denominator is in the numerator, so we are left with $\frac{1}{2}x$.

Alternatively, we could have also done something like this: $\frac{x}{y}$ as a possible answer because $y=\frac{1}{2}y*y$, and therefore, the denominator has $\frac{1}{2}y$ so we can cancel that out.

As you can see, simplifying fractions is all about being able to find [[Factor\|Factors]] that the numerator and the denominator have in common so we can cancel them out. 

Sometimes, it's not the whole denominator, sometimes it's just a factor of the denominator and the numerator that they have in common that you can factor out like $\frac{14}{35}$
14 and 35 are multiples of 7, so we can factor out the 7 from the numerator and denominator to get this $\frac{{7*2}}{7*5}$, the 7's [[cancel out]] and we get $\frac{2}{5}$


# Difference between addition and multiplication when simplifying fractions
$\frac{{3+x}}{4+x}$, can we simplify this fraction? NO, we cannot cancel out the x's to equal 1 since the x's are being added to something. $\frac{3+x}{4+x}\neq \frac{3}{4}+\frac{x}{x}$ or $\frac{3}{4}*{\frac{x}{x}}$.
