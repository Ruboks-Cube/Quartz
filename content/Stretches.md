#math/geometry/2d , [[Transformation]]


- In contrast to [[Dilation]] which multiplies the [[Scale Factor]] along both axes, a [[horizontal]] or [[vertical]] stretch changes the [[ordered pair\|coordinates]] of one axis while keeping the other unchanged. 
- While [[Dilation]] preserves shapes and keeps them [[similar]] stretches deform them. 
- Stretches do not preserve [[Angles]] in general, although they might preserve some [[Right Angle\|right angles]] in special circumstances. 
- Using coordinate algebra we can represent a [[horizontal]] stretch with a scale factor $n$ and keep the $y$ axis untouched. $(x',y')=(nx,y)$, where $(x,y)$ is a point in the [[preimage]]. 
	- Similarly, a [[vertical]] stretch has respect to the $x$ axis so $(x',y')=(x,ny)$.
