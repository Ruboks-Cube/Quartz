#math/geometry [[Triangle Congruence Theorems]], [[Triangle]]

- SAS ([[Line Segment\|side]] [[Angles\|Angle]] [[Line Segment\|side]]) is a [[Triangle Congruence Theorems\|Triangle congruence theorem]]

- If 2 [[Line Segment\|side lengths]] in 1 [[Triangle]] are the same as 2 side lengths in the other triangle  and the [[Angles\|Angle]] between them is the same then the 2 triangles are [[congruent]]
	- Like this: ![[Files/Images/SAS side angle side 2024-07-03 10.57.38.excalidraw]]
	- If 2 triangles have this exact situation then the 2 triangles are congruent. This is because when you connect the endpoints of line $X$ and line $Y$ you will get a new line that has the same exact length on both [[Triangle\|Triangles]], and so there will be the same [[Angles]].
