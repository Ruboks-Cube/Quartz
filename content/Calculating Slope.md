[[slope]], #math/algebra , [[Graphing Linear Equations]]


- We calculate slope with a simple expression:
- $\frac{y_{1}-y_{2}}{x_{1}-x_{2}}$

Why this works:
- We find the change in $y$ and the change in $x$. We know that this ratio always remains constant for [[Linear Equation\|Linear Equations]] because if we make a [[system of equations]] with the original linear equation and the formula, we can substitute $y_{1}$ and $y_{2}$ for what they equal when we [[solving multi variable equations in terms of another variable\|Solve the linear equation in terms of x]]. Things will start cancelling out and we are left with a constant ratio.
	- ~ We start with $\frac{y_{1}-y_{2}}{x_{1}-x_{2}}$ and simplify from there
- We use $m$ to denote slope.
- $\frac{{y_{1}-y_{2}}}{x_{1}-x_{2}}*x$
