- Subscripts in Math are used to denote variables that are related to each other. They look like the opposite of [[Exponents]]: ${} ^x {}$
- Subscript example: $_{x}$ 
	- $ In the [[Distance formula]] subscripts are used to relate the coordinates. We have $x_{1}$ and $y_{1}$ so we know that these coordinates are used in the same point. We can also write $(x_{1},y_{1})$ and $(x_{2},y_{2})$ instead of $(a,b)$ and $(c,d)$ because it's more clear that $x_{1}$ and $x_{2}$ are $x$ coordinates from difference points than $a$ and $c$.
