# What is a ratio?

## Basic Ratio explanation IMPORTANT

A ratio is how one numerical value relates to another. 

there are <mark class="hltr-yellow">15 pencils</mark> for every  <mark class="hltr-pink">1 marker.</mark>

Here we have the statement there are 15 pencils for every 1 marker. The ratio of pencils to markers is 15 to 1. So how can we use this ratio?

--- 
<mark class="hltr-red">THE RATIO DOES NOT TELL THE EXACT NUMBER OF ANYTHING!</mark> It just tells us that for every x value, there is y value.

Another way to think about it, is in a group, for every x there is y. There can be any number of groups.
[[Ratios come in groups]]
[[whole of a group]]
[[part of a group]]

How can we write ratios: We express ratios as "a to b," we can use a ":" to replace the "to", but we also write ratios as [[Fraction\|Fractions]] since both can represent groups, but [[Ratios and Fractions are different]], but are very similar since you can split fractions however you want as long as it's equal, just like ratios, it's just in ratios you're like adding the values or subtracting them, not splitting them.


#### Why use Ratios?
Well, we can ask questions like:

<mark class="hltr-green">How many pencils are there if we have 2 markers?</mark>

Since there are 15 pencils for every 1 marker, there will be $$15\cdot2$$
pencils for every 2 markers, since the amount of markers has doubled, so will the amount of pencils!

### 6.6, [[system of equations]] and solving for ratios
Apparently, we can solve for ratios using system of equations
![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240502185319.png]]
- Key idea, we can solve for ratios using systems because they are fractions
- Solution 1, my thinking
	- In this solution, we don't even think about [[substitution]] or [[Elimination]], just about the ratios
	- $\frac{x}{y}=\frac{2}{3}$
	- $y/z=\frac{7}{5}$
	- We know that ${} x:y = 2:3 {}$ and $y:z=7:5$
		- We have to find $x$ when $y=7$ to get it when $z=5$ since $y$ is what both have in common
		- We divide $\frac{7}{3}$ to get the value we multiply $3$ by to get $7$, so now we have our [[Scale Factor]]
		- Multiplying $2$ by $\frac{7}{3}$ gives $\frac{14}{3}$
		- Our value for $x$ when $z=5$ is $\frac{14}{3}$, so we have $\frac{5}{\frac{14}{3}}$
		- We simplify this down to $\frac{15}{14}$ as our value for $\frac{z}{x}$
- Solution 2, [[substitution]]
	- We use $\frac{x}{y}=\frac{2}{3}$ and $\frac{y}{z}=\frac{7}{5}$ to solve for the variables in terms of $y$
	- We get $x=\frac{2y}{3}$ and $z=\frac{5y}{7}$
	- We plug this in to $\frac{z}{x}$ to get $\frac{\frac{5y}{7}}{\frac{2y}{3}}$, simplifying through [[simplifying multi fractions]] gives $\frac{15}{14}$
		- Everything worked out, even though we didn't know for sure the $y$-terms would cancel out, [[Experimentation and Exploration are the key to solving problems, don't be afraid to take a step if you don't know what the next one will be]]
- Solution 3, [[Elimination]]
	- $\frac{x}{y}=\frac{2}{3}$ and $\frac{y}{z}=\frac{7}{5}$ is our equations
	- $y$ is in the [[Numerator]] of one of our equations and is in the [[Denominator]] of one of our equations, seeing this, we can eliminate the $y$ by multiplying both equations
		- We get $\frac{xy}{zy}=\frac{14}{15}$ 
		- [[cancel out]] the $y$ to get $\frac{x}{z}=\frac{14}{15}$
		- We notice we are being asked for $\frac{z}{x}$, not $\frac{x}{z}$, so we simply take the [[Reciprocal]] of everything the equation has by setting each side under $1$. [[Reciprocal#How to write the reciprocal\|Writing a reciprocal]]
	- Now we have $\frac{z}{x}=\frac{15}{14}$

- Solution 1
	- How can I solve this??? We know that at $10$ o'clock our minute hand and hour hand will be at an [[Acute Angle]], since it's 9 o'clock thats a [[Right Angle]]
	- We have to find a way to use our toolbox for math
	- Lets consider what it means when the minute hand and hour hand are pointing in the same exact direction, it means they are overlapping
		- This is what a clock looks like at 1:00![[Files/Images/Ratio 2024-05-02 20.06.13.excalidraw\|100]]
		- the minute hand will overlap with the hour hand some time between 5 minutes and 10 minutes, so there is a 5 minute possibility. 
		- Now lets consider how we could solve this, we can take the distance traveled of each and see when they overlap
			- We start with knowing the hour hand travels at $\frac{1}{60}$ the speed of the minute hand
			- Some time between $10:50$ and $10:55$ is when the hour hand and minute hand will overlap
			- At $10:50$ the hour hand is $\frac{50}{60}$ of the way there to $11$ 
		- From some calculations, we get that the minute hand moves $6^{\circ}$ every minute while the hour hand moves $0.5^{\circ}$ every minute
			- We let $y$ represent distance and $x$ time
		- We get the time they overlap by making equations, like a race, the hour hand moves at $y=0.5x$ while the minute hand moves at $y=6x$
			- We notice the hour hand has a $300^{\circ}$ head start so our equation for the hour hand is actually $y=0.5x+300$
		- Solving the system gives $x=54.5454$ which gives Which is $54 \frac{6}{11}$ after [[Turning repeating decimals into fractions]]
- Solution 2, book way
	- We need to find a way to describe the Direction of which a hand is pointing in at a given time
		- One way to do this is to find the "minute" of the clock at which a hand points
				- At midnight, or noon, both hands point to minute 0
				- At 1, the minute hand points to minute 0 and hour hand points to 1
				- There are 12 hours on the clock and 60 minutes, each hour on the clock corresponds to 5 minutes, $\frac{60}{12}=5$
				- At $1$ o'clock, the hour hand points at $1$ and minute hand at $0$, so the minute and hour hand are 5 minutes apart. 
				- Now we have a mathematical way to measure where hand points are, we need to find at what minute our hands point in the same direction
	- The minute hand points at minute $m$, we know the hour hand is between $10$ oclock and $11$ oclock, but we need to know exactly where it is pointing
		- We have to figure out what minute the hour hand is pointing to when the minute hand points at $m$
			- Start by figuring how the hour hand moves
				- The minute hand moves 60 minutes, the hour hand moves 1 hour, which is 5 minutes in distance, hour hand moves $\frac{1}{12}$ distance the minute hand moves
				- $\frac{Distance|Hour|Hand|Moves}{Distance|Minute|Hand|Moves}=\frac{1}{12}$ 
		- We know that $m$ minutes after $10$, the hour had moves $\frac{m}{12}$ minutes past 10
		- At $10$ o'clock the hour hand is pointing at $10*5$ minutes which is $50$ minutes
			- $m$ minutes after $10$ o'clock hour hand is pointing at $50+\frac{m}{12}$ minutes
		- Minute hand is pointing at $m$ minutes so we have $50+\frac{m}{12}=m$, we set the distance the hour hand travels to the distance minute hand travels
	- We solve the equation to get $54 \frac{6}{11}$
	- the hands of the clock point in the same direction at $10:54 \frac{6}{11}$
