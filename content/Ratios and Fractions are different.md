#math/algebra/inequality/equations/Ratios_Proportions_Rates 

We know that we can represent a [[Ratio]] as a [[Fraction]] with [[Parts to a Whole]]. But can we represent all ratios with a fraction?

We see fractions representing ratios all the time, in [[Rate]]s and in [[Conversion Factors]].

Ratios are very similar to fractions already, with [[Proportion]] and [[equivalent\|Equivalency]], they must be the same somehow?? And what about [[Rate]]s they are represented as fractions as well???

Even in [[Percentages\|#math/fraction/percentages]], we see percent as "per 100," and percents are just [[Fraction]]s as well! Since when you multiply a percent by something else, you get the corelating [[Fraction]] or [[decimal]] of it.

There is no innate relationship between ratios and fractions other than parts to a whole, they are just represented this way because [[equivalent]] and [[Proportion]] is very similar.
