#math/geometry 

- A [[line]] goes on for infinity, a line segment is finite
- ! A line segment has a measurable length.
- The [[Definition]] of a line segment is a part of a [[line]] that is bound by 2 endpoints and contains every point possible between the 2 endpoints. 
- Whenever you join 2 [[point\|points]] together you create a line segment.
	- When the line segment is part of a [[2D shape]] it's referred to as a side length.
- 2 basic properties of a line segment that can be compared to any other line segment is it's length and orientation. The length is just the [[Distance]] between the 2 endpoints. The orientation is just the way it's "going towards" like it it were a line where are the arrows facing.
