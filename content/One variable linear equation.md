# What is a one variable linear equation?

A one variable linear equation means that there is only one variable, though it may appear multiple times, and the term linear means that it only appears in the first [[Exponents\|Exponent]] of the [[variable math\|variable]]. 


## Problems

### A very basic linear equation

![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240415193327.png]]
We can make an [[equations\|Equation]] out of this. $x-3=6$. where $x$ is how many books *we* have.
We can use a numberline:
![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240415194513.png]]


We can also use [[inspection]]: My sister has 3 fewer books than I do. Which means I have 3 more books than her, she has 6 books, 6+3 books = 9!

We can also [[using algebra\|use algebra]] to figure it out. Our equation is $x-3=6$, we can manipulate the equation to have $x=$something.    We can get rid of the 3 on the left side by adding 3 on both sides to get $x=9$.

> [!Question] Why can we do this?
> We can think of the equation as a scale, where both sides of the scale are the same, hence it's equal. If we add 3 to both sides of the scale, it will stay the same, which is why we have the ability to do this.
> What we did here is known as [[Inverse Operation]] in order to [[isolating variables\|Isolate the variable]]

We can [[checking your answer\|Check our answer]] through plugging it in.

##### First one!
![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240428153143.png]]
- We convert this word math problem into just a math problem
	- Let $c$ represent the points the cougars scored and $p$ the points the panthers scored
	- $c+p=34$ since the cougars and panthers scored a total of 34 points
	- $p+14=c$ since the cougars won by 14 points

	- Our [[ordered pair]] is $(24,10)$
	- The panther scored 10 points

##### 5.11 quarters and nickels
![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240428154200.png]]
- First define variables
	- Let $n$ represent the number of nickels and $q$ represent amount of quarters
	- $0.05n+0.25q=9.15$ is our first equation
		- We can get rid of the decimals by multiplying by $100$
		- $5n+250q=915$ 
		- Divide by 5 to get $n+5q=183$, a much nicer equation
	- for our second equation we have to find out the weight of $1$ nickel, and $1$ quarter
	- we have $\frac{n}{90}+\frac{q}{80}=1$, we can multiply by 720 to get rid of the fractions
		- We have $8n+9q=720$
	- From our first equation, we have $n=183-5q$
		- Plug this into our second one and we have $8(183-5q)+9q=720$
		- Expanding and simplifying the left side gives $1464-31q+9q=720$
		- Solving this gives $q=24$
		- Plug this into $n=183-5q$ to get $n=183-5(24)$ which gives us $n=63$, so the bank has $63$ nickels




## Story Problems 
![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240429175051.png]]
- Let $c$ be carols age now, let $g$ be genes age  Now
	- Two years ago, gene was nine times as old as Carol, so $(g-2)=9(c-2)$, we subtract each age by $2$ because this was 2 years ago
		- Notice a bogus solution here:
			- ![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240429175250.png]]
			- We actually use $g$ and $c$ as ages 2 years ago, which is not the correct answer here
	- "He is now seven times as old as she is", so we have $g=7c$
	- Substituting $g=7c$ into our first equation we have $(7c-2)=9(c-2)$
		- Distributing the $9$ gives $7c-2=9c-18$
		- Combining like terms gives $-2c=-16$
		- Dividing by $-2$ gives $c=8$, so Carol's age is $8$
	- We plug $8$ into $g=7c$ to get $g=56$ 
	- Gene is $56$ and Carol is $8$, we use this to answer the question now
		- We let $t$ represent the number of years until Gene is 5 times older than carol
		- We have $(56+t)=5(8+t)$, this is a normal [[Linear Equation]], solving gives $t=4$, we can check our answer by plugging it in to get $60=40+20=60$, so it will be 4 years until Gene is 5 times as old as Carol
	- [[variables as unknown values\|Whenever there is an unknown value, a variable is needed!]]
