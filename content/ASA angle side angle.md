#math/geometry/2d/shape/Triangle [[Triangle]], [[Geometry]]

- ASA is a [[Triangle Congruence Theorems\|Triangle congruence theorem]] 


- If 2 [[Angles]] of the [[Triangle]] and the [[Line Segment\|side length]] connecting them on the [[preimage]] and the [[image (transformation)]] is the same then the 2 triangles are [[congruent]].
- Here is an image that demonstrates this:                                                     ![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240703100841.png|300]]
	- In the image $\angle D$, $\angle F$ and $\overline {DF}$ are all the same to their corresponding parts on $\triangle {ABC}$ so these 2 triangles are congruent
- ? Why does this work?
	- There are 2 angles and the distance between them are the same on both triangles. If we imagine a [[Ray]] coming from both angles like this: ![[Files/Images/ASA angle side angle 2024-07-03 10.10.40.excalidraw\|200]]
	- Then the rays will [[Intersect]] at the same point because it's the same [[Distance]] and same angle.
