#math/graphing #math/algebra/inequality/equations/polynomial 

- The number of times a [[Factor]] in a [[factored form]] [[polynomials\|polynomial]] appears
	- (The [[Exponents\|exponent]] of a factor) E.G $(x-1)(x+2)^2(x-3)$
		- $(x-1)$ has a multiplicity of $1$ while $(x+2)^2$ has a multiplicity of $2$ 
	- An [[Even]] multiplicity means the line will **NOT** go through the [[axis]], and will only touch it at the [[intercept\|x intercept]]
	- An [[odd]] multiplicity means the line **will** go through the [[axis]] 
- ? WHY does this happen
	- [[Sign changes in Factored Form]] → First understand
	- Now, why does the [[Multiplicity]] matter?
		- If we have an even multiplicity, the sign before crossing the root is the same, so once cross the root, the line will continue immediately in the same direction (sign is same regardless because of even [[Exponents\|exponent]]) 
		- If we have an odd multiplicity, the opposite happens because we get a sign change
