#math/geometry 

- A pair of parallel lines are lines that if they went on and on forever they would never [[Intersect]], ever, for INFINITY AND BEYOND!

	- It is possible for 2 lines to be not parallel and still not [[Intersect]] if they are on another [[plane]].
- Civil engineers often design rows of streets running in the same direction such that they don't intersect. These streets can be represented on a map as parallel lines. Engineers can create intersections, one after another, by passing a perpendicular line through a set of parallel lines. ![[Files/Images/Pasted image 20240627072736.png|400]]


- Parallel lines never intersect, and this has an interesting consequence. Parallel lines always have the same [[Distance]] between them and any [[line]] that [[Intersect\|Intersects]] one of them also intersects the other.
