- Sine and Cosine of the [[Complementary Angles]] of a [[Right Triangle]]
	- If $A$ and $B$ are the angles adjacent to the [[Hypotenuse]] of a [[Right Triangle]] then $\sin \angle A =\cos\angle B$ and $\cos \angle A=\sin\angle B$ 
	- This is because when we take the sine of $\angle A$ we do opposite over [[Hypotenuse]], for the cosine of $\angle B$ we take the [[adjacent]] over [[Hypotenuse]]. The side adjacent to $\angle B$ is the side opposite to $\angle A$. 
	- This relationship can lead to these statements 
		- For angle $\angle A$ we have:
			- $\sin \angle A = \cos (90-A)$
			- $\cos \angle A = \sin(90-A)$
