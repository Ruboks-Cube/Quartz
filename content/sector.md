#math/geometry/2d/shape/circle , [[Circle]], [[arc]], [[radius]]

- A sector is a region bounded by an [[arc]] and the 2 [[radius\|radii]] that form the [[central angle]] of that [[arc]] 
	- Both the [[arc length]] and area of the sector depend on the [[Angles\|angle]] of the [[central angle]] 
- ? What is the [[Area]] of a sector?
	- The sector area to the circle area is in the same ratio as the central angle to $360^{\circ}$
		- $\frac{\text{Sector Area}}{Circle Area}=\frac{\text{central angle}}{360^{\circ}}$
	- Rearranging the ratio and plugging in $\pi r^2$ for the area of the circle gives:
		- $\text{Sector Area}=\frac{\text{Central Angle}}{360^{\circ}}*\pi r^2$ 
	- It really makes sense because we take the fraction of the circle we have         ($\frac{\text{Central Angle}}{360^{\circ}}$) and multiply it by the original area
